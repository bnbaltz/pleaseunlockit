Ans.3 The Balance of Trade is important to calculate country’s GDP. GDP increases when Exports exceeds Imports. Then this is a trade surplus. If the Imports exceeds Exports then a trade deficit occurs and this decreases the GDP.

 U.S. have the largest trade deficits, as they are dependent on oil imports and consumer products. Japan produces and exports most of the world’s consumer products and as has recorded a trade surplus.

A country that has positive net exports enjoys a trade surplus, while negative net exports says the nation has a trade deficit.A low currency exchange rate makes a coutry's exports more price competitive.

A trade deficit is neither entirely good or bad.A trade deficit can be a sign of a strong economy & under certain conditions, can lead to stronger economic growth for the deficit-running country in the future. This might hurt job creation and economic growth in the deficit-running country.

A trade surplus is harmful only when the government uses protectionism. A trade deficit can benefit in the short-term for nations that must import heavily as an investment in economic development.
