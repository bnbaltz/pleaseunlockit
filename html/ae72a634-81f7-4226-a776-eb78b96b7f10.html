QUESTION:

I have a 10 V battery, 3 resistors with fixed resistances (4 Ω, 5 Ω, 8 Ω), 1 resistor which can be varied from 0.5 Ω – 4.5 Ω), and 1 kid's toy (with its own internal resistance of 5 Ω) which requires 1.7±0.2 A of current. [If there is too little current, the toy can't run. If there is too much current, an important circuit in the toy will burn out.]

  1. a)  Design the circuit which meets the current requirement. Restrictions:

    1. The battery, all three fixed resistors, the resistor which can be varied, and the kid's toy must all be used in the circuit.

    2. Some current (a non-negligible amount) must flow through each component. (No sparking or bleeding of current into the air, ...)

    3. Represent the toy in the circuit by a “T” in a circle, and the variable resistor by a “R” in a circle. Make sure you explicitly write the resistance selected for “R”.

  2. b)  At the last minute a 6 Ω resistor is placed from one end of the toy to one end of the 4 Ω resistor. (The variable resistor has the same value as set in part (a).) Draw the new circuit. (There are at least four correct answers given the answer to (a); put down only one of them.)

  3. c)  What is the new current running through the toy?
