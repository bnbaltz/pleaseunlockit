Answer 2. A) Apothecium- It's a bowl-shaped ascocarp or fruiting body, which contains asci and helps in sexual reproduction in Ascomycota.

B) Asci- These are sac-like structures iin Ascomycota which produce sexual spores by meiosis.

C) Ascospore- It is the sexual spore in the fungi class Ascomycota, produced endogenously in sac-like fruiting bodies called asci.

D) Hyphae- These are filamentous structures which compose the body of a fungi.

E) Mycelium- It is the collection of hyphae and the vegetative part of a fungi.

Answer 3. Sac fungi belong to the Ascomycota class.

They are characterized by sac-like sexual structures called asci, which endogenously produce ascospores.

They can be unicellular (yeast) or multicellular (penicillin, etc.)

Asexual spores called conidia are produced exogenously on conidiophores.

Their mycelium is branched and septate.

Answer 5. Fungi can reproduce sexually or asexually by spores. Asexual reproduction is by spores like conidia, sporangiospores or zoospores, while sexual reproduction happens through oospores, ascospores or basidiospores.

Sexual spores are formed by the process of meiosis, while asexual spores are formed through the process of mitosis.
