QUESTION:

The experimental magnetic moment of Na2CoCl4 is 4.59BM. The difference of the experimental and the spin only magnetic moment values can be explained by (give reason for each options)

(a.) mixing of terahedral and octahedral complexes.

(b.) mixing of ground state with first exited state.

(c.) mixing of terahedral and square planar complexes.

(d.) Jahn-Teller Distortion.

ANSWER:

Experimental Magnetic moment of Na2CoCl4 = 4.59 BM 

Answer : (b.) mixing of ground state with first exited state.

 

CoCl4-2 has a tetrahedral structure. 

Co is in +2 oxidation state (3d7) 

In tetrahedral crystal field , splitting of Co+2 is, e4 t23 

Number of unpaired electrons (n) : 3 

Theoretical spin only magnetic moment value for CoCl4-2 is √n(n+2)  = 3.88 BM 

Due to spin-orbit coupling and mixing of ground state and 1st excited state, effective magnetic moment ( μeff ) is, 

μeff = μs.o. [1- (α λ /Δ)]

where,

α is a constant (2 for an E term, 4 for an A2 term)

λ is the spin-orbit coupling constant 

For, CoCl4-2  , ground state in the tetrahedral field is split from a 4F to a 4A2 term .

For an A term the constant α = 4. 

The spin-orbit coupling constant, λ for the free Co+2 ion is -172 cm-1  

And an approximate Δ= 3100 cm-1.

Hence,

μeff = 3.88 ×[1 - {4 ×(-172)/3100}] = 4.73 BM
