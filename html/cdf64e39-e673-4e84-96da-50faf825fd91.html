QUESTION:

                    A fisherman attaches 7 hooks to his line.                 

                

                    Every time he casts the line, each hook will be swallowed by a fish with probability 0.1,                 

                

                    independent of whether any other hook is swallowed.                 

                

                    Define F to be the number of fish that are hooked on a single cast of the line.                 

                

                    (a) F is a ______________(, (___ ) random variable.                 

                

                    That is, identify the type of random variable and its parameter(s).                 

                

                    (b) What is the probability of catching 7 fish on a single cast of the line?                 

                

                    
                

                

                    (c) What is the probability of catching 3 or 4 fish on a single cast?                 

                

                    
                

                

                    (d) Now suppose the fisherman wants to catch at least one fish on each cast with 94% probability.                 

                                     What is the smallest number of hooks that achieves his goal? Note that your answer must be an integer 

ANSWER:

a) Binomial random variable : B~(n,p) = B~(7,0.1)

b) Catching 7 fishes: 7C7 * 0.1^7 * 0.9^0 = 0.1^7

c) Catching 3 fishes: 7C3 * 0.1^3 * 0.9^4 = 0.0229

Catching 4 fishes: 7C4 * 0.1^4 * 0.9^3 = 2.55 x 10^-3

So, catching 3 or 4 fishes = 0.0255

d)  P(atleast 1 fish in n hooks) = 0.94 => P(0 fishes in n hooks) = 1 - 0.94 = 0.06

=> nC0 * 0.1^0 * 0.9^n = 0.06 =>0.9^n = 0.06 => n = 26.70 ~27
