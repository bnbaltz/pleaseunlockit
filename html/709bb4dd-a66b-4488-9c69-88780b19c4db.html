ANSWER:

a) Saturated surface dry condition SSD of aggregates:

In saturated surface dry condition of aggregates, the voids in the aggregates are filled with moisture but the main surface area of the aggregate particles is dry.

b) Absorption of aggregates:

Absorption is the moisture content in the SSD condition. In moist condition, the moisture content will be in excess of the SSD condition. The difference between the actual moisture condition and the moisture content in the SSD condition is the free moisture in the aggregate.

c) Free water on aggregates:

In SSD condition, the voids in the aggregates are filled with moisture and the main area is dry. In moist aggregates, the moisture content is excess of SSD condition. Free water is the difference between the actual moisture content and the moisture content in the SSD condition.

d) The moisture captured in the voids in the aggregates is not available to react with the cement. Therefore, the water at the surface of the aggregate only reacts with the cement in the PCC mix.
