QUESTION:

One of your co-workers has started using a solvent mixture of toluene (TLV® = 20 ppm) and xylene (TLV® = 100 ppm). As part of the team in charge of the risk assessment and management program in your company, you were tasked to conduct personal air monitoring throughout a work shift and have come up with the following results:

a. Assuming that there was no dermal exposure and that the toxic effects contributed by each solvent are additive, calculate the TWA exposure for each chemical. 

b. Has the TLV®–TWA been exceeded? 

ANSWER:

The TLV -TWA is Threshhold limit Value -Time weighted average

It is the limit value of a chemical to which a worker can be exposed daily after working for lifetime without adverse health effects.

 

Part (a):

 

TWA = Σ ci* ti / Σ ti

 

Calculating TWA for Cxylene

Substituting the sampling time and concentration values from the table :

TWA = ( 60 * 2 + 92 * 2 + 0 *1 + 110 * 3 ) / (2+2+1+3) =  79.25 ppm or (79 ppm after rounding off )

 

Calculating TWA for CToluene

Substituting the sampling time and concentration values from the table :

TWA = ( 25 * 2 + 45 * 2 + 0 *1 + 47 * 3 ) / (2+2+1+3) =  35.125 ppm or (35 ppm after rounding off )

 

Part (b) :

Industrially acceptable concentrations for 8 hours TWA are

TLV - TWA for CXylene = 100 ppm

TLV - TWA for CToluene = 50 ppm

 

In the calculation both the TWA values are in the acceptable limits. The TLV-TWA has not been exceeded.
