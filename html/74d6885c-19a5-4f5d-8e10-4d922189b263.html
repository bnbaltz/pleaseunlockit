Functional Areas Of Amazon

An organizational structure which establishes the design and system of interactions among members of the firm. For e.g , Amazon’s corporate structure determines how managers direct and influence operational activities in various business areas.

The Amazon.com has a functional organizational structure. The following characteristics are the most significant in Amazon’s corporate structure:

i) Global function-based groups
ii) Global hierarchy
iii) Geographic divisions

The Global Function-Based Groups : These Function based groups are the most significant characteristic of Amazon’s organizational structure. And each business function has a team along with a senior manager. The following are the major function based groups in Amazon’s organizational structure:

    a) Office of the CEO
    b) Business Development
   c)  Amazon Web Services
    d) Finance
    e) International Consumer Business
    f) Accounting
    g) Consumer Business
    h) Legal and Secretariat

Global Hierarchy : The Global hierarchy is a traditional structural characteristic. Amazon's such characteristic is expressed in terms of a global system of vertical lines of command and authority that influence the online retail firm. Thus, the main strategic objective of the corporate structure is to facilitate managerial control of Amazon’s entire organization.

The Geographic Divisions : In Amazon’s geographic divisions groups are  based on geographic regions and related business goals. For e.g , Amazon.com uses geographic divisions to make it easier to manage the e commerce business based on economic conditions of certain regions. And the main strategic objective in having this is to enable the company to address issues relevant to each geographic region considering the differences among regional markets. Thus , Amazon has a simple approach involving the following geographic divisions in its operations: North America and International.

The Analysis by functional areas and Business Units in achieving strategic and operational goals is that if the company wants to make Alliance , Product and market development and diversification than strategic planning process is required on the part of a company’s upper-level management. In the end, a company’s management will settle on a strategy that is most likely to produce positive results (usually defined as improving the company’s bottom line) and that can be executed in a cost efficient manner with a high likelihood of success while avoiding undue financial risk.

The development and execution of strategic planning are typically viewed as consisting of being performed in three critical steps:

a) Strategy Formulation :

In the process of formulating a strategy, a company will first assess its current situation by performing an internal and external audit. And its purpose of this is to help identify the organization’s strengths and weaknesses, as well as opportunities and threats (SWOT Analysis). As a result of the analysis, managers decide on which plans or markets they should focus on or abandon, how to best allocate the company’s resources, and whether to take actions such as expanding operations through a joint venture or merger.

Business strategies have long-term effects on organizational success. Only upper management executives are usually authorized to assign the resources necessary for their implementation.

b) Strategy Implementation :

After a strategy is formulated, the company needs to establish specific goals related to putting the strategy into action and allocate resources for the strategy’s execution. The success of the implementation stage is often determined by how good a job upper management does in regard to clearly communicating the chosen strategy throughout the company .The effective strategy implementation involves developing a solid structure for implementing the strategy, maximizing the utilization of relevant resources and redirecting marketing efforts in line with the strategy’s goals and objectives.

c) Strategy Evaluation :

Strategy evaluation involves three crucial activities i.e reviewing the internal and external factors affecting the implementation of the strategy, measuring performance and taking corrective steps to make the strategy more effective.

All three steps in strategic planning occur within three hierarchical levels: upper management, middle management and operational levels. Thus, it is imperative to foster communication and interaction among employees and managers at all levels so as to help the firm to operate as a more functional and effective team.
