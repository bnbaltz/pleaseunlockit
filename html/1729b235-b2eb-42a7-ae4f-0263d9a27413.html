ANSWER

The pH of a solution with a H+ concentration of 1.00 x 10-4 M is 4.

EXPLANTION

The pH of a solution is the negative of the logarithm of the hydrogen ion concentration in the solution.

 

Formula to calculate the pH of a solution from a known [H+] is-

pH = -log [H+]

So, here H+= 1.00 x 10-4 M

Putting the value of hydrogen ion in the formula-

pH = -log [1.00 x 10-4 M]

=4
