QUESTION:

Which one of the following statements about rate constant k is FALSE?

A) k is dependent on concentration of the reactants
B) k increases with increasing temperature
C) k decreases with increasing activation energy
D) k can be increased by adding a catalyst
E) k is directly proportional to the rate of a reaction

I would like the answer and a detailed explanation please

ANSWER:

From the Arrhenius equation, rate constant k = A exp (-activation energy/RT), where A is the preexponential factor, R is the molar gas constant and T is the temperature.

From the equation, k increases when T increases (B is TRUE).

From the equation, k decreases when activation energy increases (C is TRUE).

Adding a catalyst decreases the activation energy, so k increases (D is TRUE).

From the rate equation, Rate of reaction = k x concentration of reactant to the power of the reaction order.

So k is directly proportional to the rate of reaction (E is TRUE).

The statement that is FALSE is A) k is dependent on concentration of the reactants. There is no relationship between the rate constant and reactant concentration.
