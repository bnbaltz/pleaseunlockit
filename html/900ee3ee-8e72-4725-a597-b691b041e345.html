Recording Long-term Assets

A long-term asset is recorded at the price it is purchased and includes the expenditures that were incurred to own the asset. This means that the purchase price is not the only expenditure incurred, there are other expenditures like transportation, property taxes. Few examples of long-term assets are land, land improvements, buildings, equipment and even natural resources.

The company purchased land, building and equipment all for $400,000. The fair values were $150,000, $300,000 and $50,000 which means that the total fair value is $500,000. The amounts that will be recorded in each of the accounts is computed below.

The amounts that will be recorded in each of the accounts is computed below.

Explanation

Based on the total fair value, allocated percentage of each asset is computed. As the company paid $400,000 for the assets, this has been multiplied by the allocated percentage of each asset. This amount is the recorded amount in the books of company.
