QUESTION:

Jill just opened a savings account with Santander Bank and deposited $2,000 into it. The account pays an annual nominal interest of 1%, but interest is compounded every month. If Jill doesnâ€™t make any future deposits or withdrawals, what will her account balance be after 3 years (ignore all service charges and taxes)?

ANSWER:

Here we will use the following formula:

FV = PV * (1 + r%)n

where, FV = Future value, PV = Present value = $2000, r = rate of interest = 1% compounded monthly. So monthly rate = 1% / 12 = 0.08333%, n= time period = 3 * 12 = 36 months

now, putting theses values in the above equation, we get,

FV = $2000 * (1 + 0.0833%)36

FV = $2000 * (1 + 0.000833)36

FV = $2000 * (1.000833)36

FV = $2000 * 1.03044165927

FV = $2060.8833

So, account balance after 3 years will be $2060.88
