QUESTION:

 

  1. The following scenario will provide information that you will need in order to use the scientific method to determine how you would be able to identify an organism.

 

                 The scenario is:

                 A patient is in the hospital with bacterial meningitis. The doctor needs to determine whether the patient has an infection with Neisseria meningitidis or Haemophilus influenzae.

 

                 Using the scientific method, address the following:  (30 points)

                

 

  1. What is the problem (scenario) that you are asked to solve? (7 points)

 

  1. What is your hypothesis for the solution to the problem? (What organism do you think caused the problem?) (7 points)

 

  1. What are the experiments that are required to answer your hypothesis?  (The stains, biochemical, and media for each organism and the expected results) (8 points)

 

  1. What is your conclusion to the problem? (Based on the expected results, what organism caused the problem?) (8 points)

ANSWER:

Question 1

Scenario(problem)

A case of unknown bacterial meningitis got admitted to the hospital

Question 2

Organisms (hypothesis)

Doctor assumed that it is one of the two organisms - Neisseria meningitidis or Hemophilus influenzae which caused the meningitis.This hypothesis would have been made based on the age of the patient and clinical findings. The hypothesis need to be verified using microbiological investigations in CSF sample.

Question 3

Experiments required:

For Neisseria meningitidis-

Gram stain: Gram positive diplococci,0.8 microns size, with adjacent side flattened(half moon shaped),and is capsulated. Pus cells are also seen .The organism is seen inside the pus cells and also extracellularly.

Culture media- organism is fastidious and will not grow in basal media.Needs blood agar and chocolate agar.Incubate at 37 degree Celsius for 24-48 hrs at 5-10% carbondioxide.Colonies will be small(1mm) round,convex,grey, translucent after 24 hrs.After 48 hrs,colonies will be larger, with opaque raised centres and thin transparent margins. In blood agar, hemolysis is also seen.

Biochemical tests:

Catalase positive, Oxidase positive 

Rapid carbohydrate utilization test: ferments glucose and maltose,  but not sucrose. 

For Hemophilus influenzae:

Gram stain: Pleomorphic Gram negative coccobacilli with pus cells

Culture media: Organism is highly fastidious. Needs Factors X and V present in blood for growth. Largely aerobic. Growth is enhanced by 5-10% carbondioxide.

Blood agar with Staphylococcus aureus streak: Colonies are grey moist, those nearer to streak line are larger than those away from the line(satellitism)

Chocolate agar: similar growth 

Transparent media such as Fildes agar and Levinthal's agar: Iridescent colonies 

Hemophilus selective media having bacitracin and sucrose : Hemophilus parainfluenza will ferment sucrose, Hemophilus influenzae will not. 

Biochemical tests:

Catalase positive and oxidase positive 

Reduce nitrate to nitrite 

Ferments glucose, xylose but not lactose, sucrose, mannitol. 

Disk test for X and V requirements: Growth is seen around X ,V and XV disc in media lacking these factors. 

Question 4

Conclusion:

If the results shows Gram negative capsulated half moon shaped diplococci, producing small ,grey, translucent colonies which becomes larger,opaque on further incubation, catalase and oxidase positive, fermenting glucose, maltose,conclude that Neisseria meningitidis is the causative organism 

If the results show Gram negative pleomorphic coccobacilli, producing grey moist colonies with satellitism,iridescent colonies on transparent media, disk test showing growth around X,V and XV disk ,catalase and oxidase positive, fermenting glucose and xylose,reduce nitrate to nitrite, conclude that the organism is Hemophilus influenzae.
