Question 2:  A new brand will be necessary to support this target market.  This necessity is driven largely by the influence of cannibalization.  If certain ingredients can be removed from an existing product to make the new formula cheaper, the overall profits for Unilever will stay largely unchanged.  Loyal consumers will stay with their higher quality and higher price brands.  More price-concerned consumers will transition to the new brand, while the switching is offset by the increased profit margins from the formula change.  The break-even for a new introduction will likely have to occur over the long term, as the promotion will have to be completed extremely strategically.  Assuring consumers can clearly distinguish between each brand without negatively affecting the continued development of the Unilever brand is vital, and marketing initiatives will be the way Unilever communicates these messages, thus costs will be higher in the short-run to manage this.  Once consumer expectations and understanding have settled, marketing costs can reduce and break-even from brand introduction will be achieved.  The new product would be positioned as a higher quality, low cost detergent aimed at bridging the gap between Campeiro and Minerva brands.  It will satisfy the quality needs presented by low-income consumers, while staying at a low cost.  Canabalization should be avoided through this strategically designed decision and result in a growth of overall market share and profitability.  The name to use will be “Key”, taken from the African region and can be used to cater to the African roots present in the country.
