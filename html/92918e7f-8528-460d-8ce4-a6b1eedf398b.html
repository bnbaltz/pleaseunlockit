ANSWER:

To understand the effect of transactions on current ratio and acid test ratio (i.e. quick ratio), it is neccessary to know that what these ratios are:

  • Current Ratio = current assets / current liabilities
  • Acid test Ratio = Quick assets / current liabilities
  • Quick Assets = Current assets - inventory

Now,

1. Provide services to customer on account:

This transaction means that services are being provided on credit.

The entry for this transaction would be trade receivable account (sundry debtor) debit and services revenue account will be credited with the same amount.

Where, trade receivable account is included in current assets, quick assets and services revenue account is a revenue account.

Since, trade receivables are debited, it will increase the amount of trade receivables and also will increase the total amount of current assets, quick assets and change in revenue is not to be taken in our ratios.

According to mathematics rule, if in a ratio, numerator increases and denominator remains the same the ratio will increase.

Hence, with the increase in current assets, quick assets and no change in current liabilities, the current ratio and acid test ratio will increase.

 

2. Borrow cash from the bank by signing a long-term note payable.

The entry for this transaction would be cash account debit and long term loan account credit with the same amount.

Cash is included in current asset, quick assets and long term loan is long term borrowings.

Now, our current assets as well as quick assets will increase and there will be no effect on current liabilities.

According to mathematics rule, if in a ratio, numerator increases and denominator remains the same the ratio will increase.

Hence, with the increase in current assets, quick assets and no change in current liabilities, the current ratio and acid test ratio will increase.

 

3. Purchase office supplies with cash:

The entry for this transaction would be office supplies account debit and cash account credit with the same amount.

Where office supplies are included in current assets, quick assets and cash is also included in current assets, quick assets.

Since, office supplies and cash are both part of current assets, quick assets; and will be debited and credited with same amount which will not change the total of current assets and quick assets.

Hence, there will be no change in current ratio as well as acid test ratio ratio.

 

4. Pay rent for the current period:

The entry for this transaction would be rent account debit and cash/bank account credit with the same amount.

Where, rent account is an expense account and cash/bank account is included in current assets, quick assets.

Since, cash/bank account is credited it will reduce the casdh/bank balance with which the total of current assets,quick assets will be reduced.

According to mathematics rule, if in a ratio, numerator decreases and denomenator remains unchanged, the resulting ratio would be less than the original.

Hence, with the decrease in current assets, quick assets; the current ratio and acid test ratio will decrease.

  1. current ratio and acid test ratio will increase.

  3. current ratio and acid test ratio will remain the same.
  4. current ratio and acid test ratio will decrease.
