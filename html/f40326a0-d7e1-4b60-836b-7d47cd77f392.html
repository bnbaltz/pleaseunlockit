QUESTION:

How has Unilever targeted the black population in Brazil? How does the company’s strategy in Brazil differ from its strategy in other countries? What does your response tell you about Unilever’s overall global marketing strategy?

ANSWER:

Brazil is home to a sizable racial minority. Brazilians think of themselves as part of a culture that transcends race. Unilever initially thought that the African segment transcended national borders of America & Africa. Unilever marketed its products, specifically targeted at black people in the same way it was effective in US. But black Brazilians did not response well to this. Unilever then understands that because Brazil’s blacks think of themselves as falling into a range of skin tones rather than simply black and hence Unilever approach to the Brazilian market has been to target the entire population rather than certain segment. New Campaign featured 3 women with different skins. Product meant for “tan and black skin” which could include many white women. New strategy correctly defined Brazilians as a part of a culture that transcends color or race. This strategy shows that Unilever is using localization approach for its marketing. Basically unilever strategy for Global market is to select some features to include in all its campaigns while localizing other features. Save some costs, customize to different cultures while still having consistent brand image.
