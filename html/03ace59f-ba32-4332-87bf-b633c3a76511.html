The weekly amount of money spent on maintenance and repairs by a company was observed, over a long period of time, to be approximately normally distributed with mean $420 and standard deviation $10. How much should be budgeted for weekly repairs and maintenance so that the probability the budgeted amount will be exceeded in a given week is only 0.05? (Round your answer to the nearest cent.)

ANSWER:

 = 420,  = 10

We convert this to standard normal as 

P( X < x) = P(Z < x -  / ) 

We have to calculate x, such that 

P( X > x) = 0.05

That is 

P( Z > x -  / ) = 0.05

P( Z < x -  / ) = 1 - 0.05

P( X < x -  / ) = 0.95

From the z table, look for z-score whose probability is 0.95, we get z = 1.645

x -  /  = 1.645

Put the values of   and  in above equation and solve for x

x - 420 / 10 = 1.645 

x = 436.45

Budgeted amount = $436.45
