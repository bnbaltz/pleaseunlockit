Selective precipitation

First calculate molar solubility of the two salts,

Ksp for Li2CO3 = [Li+]^2.[CO3^2-]

8.2 x 10^-4 = (2x)^2.(x)

x = 0.059 M

Ksp for LiF = [Li+][F-]

1.8 x 10^-3 = (x)(x)

x = 0.042 M

So Li2CO3 is more soluble than LiF

amount of [Li+] when Li2CO3 begins to precipitate,

[Li+] = sq.rt.(8.2 x 10^-4/0.19) = 0.066 M

The amount of [F-] in solution at this [Li+],

[F-] = 1.8 x 10^-3/0.066 = 0.0273 M

Percent of first ion [F-] remained in solution when second anion [CO3^2-] begins to precipitate,

 = 0.0273 x 100/0.367

 = 7.44%
