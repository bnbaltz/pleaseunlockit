**Consider that when we excersie we burn glucose and also lose mass. Why does this happen?

ANSWER:

2.Reducing the number of protons required by ATP synthase to produce an ATP molecule

4H+ ions are required for the synthesis of one molecule of ATP. If somehow the proton number is decreased, probably the number of ATP synthesized could be more.  

As the electrons travel through the chain, they go from a higher to a lower energy level, moving from less electron-hungry to more electron-hungry molecules. Energy is released in these “downhill” electron transfers, and several of the protein complexes use the released energy to pump protons from the mitochondrial matrix to the intermembrane space, forming a proton gradient. The transport chain builds a proton gradient across the inner mitochondrial membrane, with a higher concentration of H+ in the intermembrane space and a lower concentration in the matrix. This gradient represents a stored form of energy, and, as we’ll see, it can be used to make ATP.
