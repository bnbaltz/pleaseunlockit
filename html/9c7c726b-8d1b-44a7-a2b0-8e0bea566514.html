One to one relationship

A One to one relationship is field which is of each one entity will be related with the zero or one field of another entity.

Here, primary key is defined as an attributes or set of attributes which is used to distinguish one entity instances from the other entity instances or identify the all entity instances uniquely. And foreign key is a referential key that refers to another table and will be primary key of that table. Foreign key is also used to make a relationship between the two tables.

Example:

The two entities PERSON and PASSPORT are in one to one relationship. As each PERSON has provided only one current and valid passport and each passport refers to single person.

In the above diagram, PASSPORT_NO is the foreign key in PERSON entity and primary key in the PASSPORT entity.

While implementing 1:1 relationship, the foreign key is located to the optional side.

Here primary key is located on the mandatory side and the foreign key should be mandatory.

Example:

The below diagram shows the CUSTOMER/ORDER relationship:

The CUSTOMER is mandatory to the ORDER as customer gives the order and order is dependent upon customer.

So that, the CUSTOMER_ID in ORDER entity will be represented as foreign key and CUSTOMER_ID in CUSTOMER entity will be represented as primary key.

One to one relationship assures that no two entity sets are located in one table.
