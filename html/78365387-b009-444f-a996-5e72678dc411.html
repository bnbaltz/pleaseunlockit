QUESTION:

The Deligne Dam on the Cayley River is built so that the wall facing the water is shaped like the region above the curve y=0.7x^2 and below the line y=220. (Here, distances are measured in meters.) The water level is 36 meters below the top of the dam. Find the force (in Newtons) exerted on the dam by water pressure. (Water has a density of 1000kg/m^3, and the acceleration of gravity is 9.8m/sec^2 .) i need to know how to do this so best explanation will be rewarded points. thank you

ANSWER:

set origin at bottom of the dam, 
we know that F=mg=p*V*g 

where 

p= density (rho) 
V=volume 
g=gravitational constant 


then 
dF=p*g*2*xi*yi*dy 

from y=.4x^2 we find x 

xi=sqrt(y/.4) 

dF=p*g*2*sqrt(y/.4)*yi*dy 

then we integrate from zero (bottom of the dam) to top of the water surface 240-40=200 


F=integral (from 0 to 200) of p*g*2*sqrt(y/.4)*yi*dy 

F=(1/sqrt(.4))*2*p*g*int(y^3/2)dy 

F=(1/sqrt(.4))*2*p*g*(y^5/2)*2/5 evaluated from 0-200 

F=(1/sqrt(.4))*2*p*g*(200^5/2)*2/5 - 0
