QUESTION:

1. To claim that a person’s autonomous choice is worthy of moral deference is to suggest that:

a. their choice is not worthy of respect 

b. we should respect the agent's decision and not intervene to prevent them from making that choice 

c. we should not say anything negative about that choice 

d. we are morally justified in exercising paternalism with regard to that choice 

 

2. Aristotle characterizes each of the virtues as a “mean between two extremes.” Which of the following is an accurate portrayal of this “rule of the Golden Mean”? 

a. Courage is a mean, or balance, between dishonesty and disloyalty.

b. A person must express extreme courage, generosity, and loyalty in order to be considered virtuous. 

c. To be courageous means to not give into one's fears completely (and be cowardly), and to not ignore one's fears completely (and be reckless), but to respond to fear in the right way, toward the right people, at the right time. 

d. Acting virtuously helps a person to achieve eudaimonia, or a flourishing life.

 

3. According to Preston-Roedder, what can the virtue of faith in humanity help us to achieve or accomplish? 

a. It can prompt the person on the receiving end of this virtue to do the right thing.

b. It can help us to give important moral support to other people

c. It can prevent us from treating other people unjustly

d. All of above

 

4. According to one of the central claims of cultural relativism,

a. each culture abides by the same universal conceptions of right and wrong. 

b. the moral code of each individual culture determines what is right and wrong for the members of that culture. 

c. the moral codes of some cultures are superior to the moral codes of others. 

d. there are universal moral truths that one finds in every culture. 

 

5. In which of the following cases does Susan Moller Okin think that multiculturalism undermines the basic principles of a liberal society? 

a. whenever a minority culture is denied a special group right 

b. in all cases: Okin believes that multiculturalism always violates the basic principles of liberal societies 

c. any time that a minority culture receives special group rights 

d. when the special group rights given to a minority culture undermine the individual rights of that culture’s members

ANSWER:

 

Ans 1. B

b. we should respect the agent's decision and not intervene to prevent them from making that choice


Ans 2. B
b. A person must express extreme courage, generosity, and loyalty in order to be considered virtuous.

Ans 3. D
d. All of above

Ans 4. B
b. the moral code of each individual culture determines what is right and wrong for the members of that culture.


Ans 5. D

d. when the special group rights given to a minority culture undermine the individual rights of that culture’s members
