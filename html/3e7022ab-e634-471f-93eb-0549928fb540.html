ANSWER:

The Grignard reagent is an organomagnesium compound formed by the reaction of magnesium metal with alkyl or aryl halide in presence of ether. These reagents are extremely good nucleophiles and are utilized in reaction with electrophiles to give addition products. Grignard reagents react with carbon dioxide, esters and aldehydes, ketones,  and even epoxides. They can be used to prepare alcohol, acid, and even carbonyl compounds.
