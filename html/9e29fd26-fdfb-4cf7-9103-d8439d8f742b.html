QUESTION:

Part 2 on “Logical Deduction” (Lecture 5 & 6)

  • For each argument, (1) decided whether it is valid or invalid, and (2) identify and explain its logical structure.

  • The following examples show the format of answers.

  • Assessment weight: 6% (1.5% for each question)

  1. Unless the deficit will be reduced, disaster will face the country. But the deficit will be reduced. So, no disaster will face the country.

  2. If you achieved happiness, then you have a lot of money. And it is impossible to have lots of money without working hard. But you simply don’t want to work hard. So, happiness is something impossible for you.

  3. Rock sample X came either from Mars or Venus. If it did not come from Mars, then it does not contain silicon. Research shows that rock sample X does not contain silicon. So, rock sample X came from Venus.

  4. If I was diligent then I will pass this exam. Unless I am happy, I will not pass this exam. If I do not celebrate then I am not happy. Therefore if I was diligent then I will celebrate.

Bonus (weight: 2%)

No individuals who laugh all the time are persons with a true sense of humour. All individuals with a true sense of humour is someone who enjoys life. So no persons who enjoy life are individuals who laugh all the time.



ANSWER:

Ans.1)
1. Invalid
2. Pattern :
P: Deficit will be reduced
Q : Disaster will face the country
a. (If not P then Q)
b. (P)
c. Therefore, (not Q)
Explanation :
" If not P then Q" implies "If not Q then P" by transposition. But for "not Q" t be the conclusion, we require the statement "If P then not Q" which is not true.So, the conclusion does not follow the premises.

Ans.2) 
1. Valid
2. Pattern :
P : You achieve happiness
Q : You work hard
R : You have lots of money
a. (If P then R)
b. (Not (R and Not Q))
c. (Not Q) 
d. Therefore, (Not P)
Explanation :
e. (Not R or Not Not Q)        De Morgan's Law, b
f. (Not R  or Q)                     Double Negation, e
g. (If R then Q)                     Material Implication, f
h. (Not R)                             Modus Tollens, g, c
i. (Not P)                               Modus Tollens, a, h
Hence, the conclusion follows from the premises.

Ans.3)
1. Invalid
2. Pattern :
P :  Rock sample X came from Mars  
Q : Rock sample X came from Venus
R : Rock sample X contains sillicon
a. (P or Q)
b. (If not P then not R)
c. (Not R)
d. Therefore, (Q)
Explanation : 
For conclusion to be "Q", we require "not P" as true. For "not P" to be True, we require "If not R then not P"to be True since "not R" is True. But "If not R then not P" is not True and hence the conclusion does not follow the premises.

Ans.4)
1. Valid
2. Pattern :
P : I was diligent then I will pass this e-xam. Unless I am happy, I will not pass this e-xam. If I do not celebrate then I am not happy. Therefore if I was diligent then I will celebrate.
Q : I will pass this e-xam
R : I am happy
S : I will celebrate
a. (If P then Q)
b. (If not R then not Q)
c. (If not S, then not R)
d. Therefore, (If P then S)
Explanation :
e. (If not S then not Q)               Hypothetical Syllogism, c, b
f. (If not not Q then not not S)    Transposition, e
g. (If Q then S)                           Double Negation, f
h. (If P then S)                            Hypothetical Syllogism, a, g
Hence, the conclusion follows from the premises.
