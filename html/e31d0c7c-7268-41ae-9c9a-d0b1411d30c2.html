given 2020 2021 2022

  1. Calculate the change in the total dollar value of transactions in nominal terms, if velocity remains constant at 14, and the money supply increases from $240 billion to $265 billion.                                                                                                                    (1 point)

 

  1. The Consumer Price Index in 2020 was 121, and by the end of 2021 the CPI is predicted to reach 129.

If the average nominal interest rate is 11.62%, compute the real rate of interest.                                                                  (1 point)

 

  1. Determine the change in the nominal GDP, if the money supply grows by 13%, and velocity falls by 20%.                 (1 point)

 

Estimate what happens to the price level, if the money supply declines from $190 billion to $175 billion, while velocity and output remain constant at 9 and $4,000 billion, respectively.                                                

ANSWER:

(1) Quantity Theory of money:

MV = PY

Where M is money supply; V is velocity; P is price level ;Y is real GDP; PY is nominal GDP. 

=> V = (PY / M)

------------

Velocity in 2021

=> V = (4400 / 430)

=> V = 10.2325

Velcoity in 2022

=> V = (4800 / 450)

=> V = 10.6667

Expected growth rate of velocity between 2021 and 2022 = [( Velocity in 2022 - Velocity in 2021) / Velocity in 2021]*100

=> Expected growth rate of velocity between 2021 and 2022 = [(10.6667 - 10.2325) / 10.2325]*100

=> Expected growth rate of velocity between 2021 and 2022 = 4.24%

-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

(2) 

Quantity Theory of money:

MV = PY

Where M is money supply; V is velocity; P is price level ;Y is real GDP; PY is nominal GDP. 

Note: PY measures the total value of transaction in nominal terms. 

---------

When  M is $240 billion

MV = PY

=> $240 billion * 14 = PY

=> PY = $3360 billion

-------

When M is $265 billion

MV = PY

=> $265 billion * 14 = PY

=> PY = $3710 billion

The change in total value of transaction in nominal terms = [(3710  - 3360) / 3360]*100

=> The change in total value of transaction in nominal terms = 10.42%

-----------------------------------------------------------

(3) Inflation rate = [(CPI in 2021 - CPI in 2020) / CPI in 2020]*100

=> Inflation rate = [(129 - 121) / 121]*100

=> Inflation rate = 6.62%

--------

Real interest rate = Nominal interest rate - Inflation rate

=> Real interest rate = 10.62% - 6.62%

=> Real interest rate = 4%

-------------------------------------------------------------------

(4) 

Quantity Theory of money:

MV = PY

Where M is money supply; V is velocity; P is price level ;Y is real GDP; PY is nominal GDP. 

In terms of growth:

% change in M + % change in V = % change in P + % change in Y

% change in M + % change in V = % change in nominal GDP

=> % change in nominal GDP = 13% + (-20%)

=> % change in nominal GDP = -7%

Note: % change in P + % change in Y = % change in nominal GDP

-------------------------------------------

(5) Money supply falls from $190 billion to $175 billion

=> % change in money supply = [(175 - 190) / 190]*100

=> % change in money supply = -7.89%

---------

The velocity and output remains constant. 

=> % change in velocity = 0

=> % change in output = 0
