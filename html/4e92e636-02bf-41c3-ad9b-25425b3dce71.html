ANSWER:

Q) Describe the arrangement of leaves in equisetum.
 Ans : 
The characteristics of leaves of the Equisetum - they are small, simple, scale-like and which are isophyllous; arranged in a whorled manner, they are attached at each node, which is united at least for a part of the length and thus form a sheath that can be seen around the stem. The sheath formed has free and pointed teeth-like tips. The leaves which are tooth like have a unbranched mid vein.

The number of leaves present per the node varies according to different species. The species which have narrow stems have few leaves ( like in E. scirpoides which have 2-3 leaves) and which have thick stem will have many leaves (like in sps E. schaffneri which upto 40 leaves).

The number of leaves present at each node corresponds to the number of ridges which is on the internode seen below. The characteristic is that these leaves do not undergo any photosynthetic function and the main function of these leaves is to provide the protection for young buds which are present at the node.
