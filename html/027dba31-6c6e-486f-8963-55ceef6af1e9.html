QUESTION:

Which of the following Fed actions will increase bank lending?

 You may select more than one answer. 

The Fed raises the discount rate from 5 percent to 6 percent.

The Fed raises the reserve ratio from 10 percent to 11 percent.

The Fed lowers the discount rate from 4 percent to 2 percent.

The Fed buys $400 million worth of Treasury bonds from commercial banks.

ANSWER:

Bank lending would increase due to:

FED lowers the discount rate from 4 percent to 2 percent.

Fed buys $400 million worth of treasury bonds from commercial banks.

Discount rate is the rate at which banks borrow from the fedreral reserve , hence a lower discount rate reduces the costs of borrowing by banks from the Fed and thus increases the amount banks would borrow from Fed.

In the 2nd case if Fed buys $400 million worth of treasury bonds from commercial banks , in return it would release $400 million to other banks.Thus the banks lending increases by $400 million.
