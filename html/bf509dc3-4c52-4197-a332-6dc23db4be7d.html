QUESTION:

Find the equation of the plane passing through points P(1,1,-2), Q(0,2,1), and R(-1,-1,0).

ANSWER:

Given points are P(1,1,-2), Q(0,2,1), and R(-1,-1,0).

The two vectors in the plane are

PQ=<0-1,2-1,1+2>=<-1,1,3>

PR=<-1-1,-1-1,0+2>=<-2,-2,2>

Normal vector to the plane is

N=<a,b,c>=vector PQ cross product of vector PR

=|i j k

-1 1 3

-2 -2 2|

=i(2+6)-j(-2+6)+k(2+2)

=8i-4j+4k

N=<a,b,c>=<8,-4,4>

The equation of the plane is

a(x-x0)+b(y-y0)+c(z-z0)=0

We take a point on the plane is (x0,y0,z0)=(1,1,-2) and normal vector N=<8,-4,4>

Substitute above values into plane equation

8(x-1)-4(y-1)+4(z+2)=0

8x-8-4y+4+4z+8=0

8x-4y+4z+4=0

The equation of the plane is 8x-4y+4z+4=0  passing through the given three points.
