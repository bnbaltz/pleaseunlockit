  1. If a crossover between two genes affects a crossover between another two genes, that interaction is called interference. It is a measurement of the independence of crossovers from each other and is calculated from double cross over.

Interference = 1 − coefficient of coincidence (c.o.c.)

Coefficient of coincidence = observed number of double cross over / expected number of double cross over.

Expected DCO

= frequency of crossing over between y and w *frequency of crossing over between w and ec = distance between y and w/100 * distance between w and ec/100

= 0.0184*0.0445 = 0.0008184 ~ 0.0008

Observed DCO = DOC/ Total number = (2+3)/1304 = 0.0038

Coefficient of coincidence = C = Observed DCO / Expected DCO = 0.0038/0.0008 = 4.75

Interference = 1 − coefficient of coincidence (c.o.c.) = 1 - 4.75 = -3.75

The interference indicates how strongly a crossover in one of the DNA regions interferes with the formation of a crossover in the other region. A positive interference meaning one crossing over reduces the chances of another crossing over in its vicinity, while a negative interference value means recombination in one location enhances the occurrence of recombination in another location.
