QUESTION:

2. Human chromosome 1 contains 247 million nucleotide pairs. The double helix measures 0.34nm/bp. (1pt each, 2pts for e)

a. How long is chromosome 1 in mm?

b. A nucleosome contains 147bp of DNA wrapped around the histone core, and 53 bp of DNA in the linker. How many nucleosomes are there on chromosome 1?

c. A nucleosome, measures 11nm in diameter (the linker is how long in diameter?) – what is the length of the chromosome when it is condensed into the ‘beads-on-a-string’ format?

d. What is the ratio of unwrapped DNA to the beads-on-a-string form of chromatin?

e. Why is it necessary to compact chromatin further into the 30nm and 300nm fibers?

ANSWER:

a. Length of chromosome 1 = 247 X 10^6 X 0.34 = 8.398 X 10^7 nm
1 nm = 10^-6 mm
= 83.98 mm

b. Number of nucleosomes = (247 X 10^6)/200
= 1.235 X 10^6 

c. Length of chromosome in beads on a string strcture = 11 X 1.235 X 10^6
= 1.3585 X 10^7 nm

d. Ratio = (8.398 X 10^7)/(1.3585 X 10^7)
= 6.18

e. DNA needs to condensed further in order accommodate it in the nucleus.
