QUESTION:

A solution is prepared by adding 4.50 mol of sodium hydroxide to 1.00 L of 1.00 M Co(NO3)2. What is the equilibrium concentration of cobalt ions? Kf = 5.0

ANSWER:

Kf (Co(OH)4-- = [Co(OH)4--] / [OH-]^4 / [Co++] 


Starting with 1 Molar Co(NO3)2, assume that Co(NO3)2 is completely disassociated into 2M NO3- and 1M Co++.


NaOH is also completely Disassociated into 4.5 M Na+ and 4.5M OH-. (At this pOH, the CobaltDinitrate would surely be disassociated) 


With a high Kf of 5.0 x 10^9, most of the 1M Cobalt will be hydroxylated taking 4M of the OH- with it, leaving 0.5M OH-. 


So the [Co(OH)4--] will be close to 1M

 
The [OH-] will be close to 0.5M and... 


The [Co++] will be solved from the Kf equation. 


5.0 x 10^9 = 1M / 0.5^4 M / [Co++] (dividing by 0.5 is the same as multiplying x 2) 
5.0 x 10^9 = 16 / [Co++] 
[Co++] = 16 / 5 x 10^9 
[Co++] = 3.2 x 10^-9 


This amount is so small that the amount does not mathematically change the concentration of the Co(OH)4-- or OH-. If it is within two significant figures of the other ions, then you will need to do more algebra, but this approximation is accurate.
