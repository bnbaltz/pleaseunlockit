QUESTION:

The current through a certain heater wire is found to be fairlyindependent of its temperature. If the current through the heaterwire is doubled, the amount of energy delivered by the heater in agiven time interval will
(a) increase by a factor of two.        (d) decrease by a factor of four.
(b) decrease by a factor of two.        (e) increase by a factor of eight.
(c) increase by a factor of four.

ANSWER:

If a current I is passing through a heater wire of resistanceR is over a time of t thn the amount of heat energy delivered fromthe heater wire is
                     Q = I2Rt
From this formula the heat energy (Q) delivered is directlyproportional to square of the current (I) passing through it.
That means  Q α I2
Then if the current (I) passing through the wire is doubledthen the heat energy delivered is increased by 4 times.
