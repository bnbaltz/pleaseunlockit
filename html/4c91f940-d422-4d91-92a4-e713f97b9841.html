QUESTION:

we know that X(bar) is approximately N(μ, σ2/n) for large n. Find the approximate distribution of μ(X bar) = X3 bar).

 

ANSWER:

Firstly, note that the question asks to *approximate* the probability that X-bar lies between 1/2 and 2/3. This would indicate to me to consider the result from the Central Limit Theorem.

Suppose that a random variable, X, comes from a distribution with mean µ and variance s². Consider taking samples from the population and recording the mean of each sample, say X-bar(1), X-bar(2), etc. Plotting these values gives the sampling distribution of X-bar. According to the Central Limit Theorem, the sampling distribution of X-bar ? N(µ, s²/n) as the size of each sample, n, approaches infinity (the number of samples is assumed to be infinite).

Excellent, we can use this result! Let X~uni(0, 1) where X is continuous. Then the mean of a continuous uniform random variable is 1/2 and the variance is 1/12 (I'll use this without proof - since you obviously know about moment generating functions, you should be able to get this result yourself anyway). Therefore, using the notation above, µ = 1/2, and s² = 1/12. If we have 12 random samples, then n = 12.

By the CLT, the sampling distribution of X-bar ? N(µ, s²/n) as n ? infinity. Since n = 12 and not infinity, we can only approximate the distribution of X-bar. Therefore the sampling distribution of X-bar is approximately:

N(µ, s²/n)
= N(1/2, (1/12)/12)
= N(1/2, 1/144)

To find the probability that X-bar lies between 1/2 and 2/3, we just need to standardise this random variable. We do this by subtracting the mean and dividing by the variance.

Let Z = (X-bar - µ) / (s²/n)
= (X-bar - 1/2) / (1/144)
= 144(X-bar - 1/2)

This random variable, Z, is now a standard normal variable with mean 0 and variance 1. That's helpful because now all you have to do is manipulate the probability expression and look up a statistics table.

P(1/2 < X-bar < 2/3)
= P{144(1/2 - 1/2) < Z < 144(2/3 - 1/2)}
= P{0 < Z < 24}
= 0.50000 using a standard normal stats table
