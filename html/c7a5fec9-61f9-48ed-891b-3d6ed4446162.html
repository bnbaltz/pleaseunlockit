Goods are produced with the purpose of being sold to the customer. The cost of goods produced but not yet sold is reported as inventory whereas costs of goods produced which are sold to customer are reported as cost of goods sold.

Goods are purchased at different cost in different point in times, makes it difficult to determine cost of goods sold. There are various methods like:

• FIFO (first in first out)

• LIFO (last in first out)

• Average cost

For valuing the inventory and thus, helps to determine cost of goods sold.

Under FIFO (first in first out) method goods which were brought first are sold first, that is cost of inventory which was first purchased or produced, will leave the inventory first and thus, become the cost of goods sold.

If 1,150 units are sold calculate cost of goods sold by using LIFO method of accounting:

Consider:

The number of units sold is 1,150

The beginning inventory is 650 units at $16 per unit.

The production during the month is 950 units at $19 per unit.

In this case under LIFO method, cost of goods sold (1,150 units) includes:

• 950 units at $19 per unit first (goods produced last are sold first )

• 200 units (total unit sold – goods produced last) at $16 per unit from the inventory at the beginning of the month.
