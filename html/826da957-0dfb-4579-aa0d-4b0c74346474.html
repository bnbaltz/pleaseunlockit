Equity investment

Equity investment refers to the worth of assets & money which the investor invests in the company for earning dividend & capital gain & entitled to the shares of the company. Equity investment is the percentage of the stock which is held by investor by investing in the company.

When the investor holds more than 50% ownership in the Investee Company then the investor has controlling influence over the company, consolidation method is used.

If the investor holds between 20% & 50%, the investor has significant influence over the company; equity method is used. If the investor has less than 20% ownership in Investee Company then the investor has insignificant influence, fair value method is used.

Fair value method is such where market price of securities is not determined & traders trade freely. In this method equity investment are classified as trading securities or available for sale securities.

As per the given fact, equity investment is classified as available for sale which are held for other than to earn profit. At the time of purchasing the investment, the investment account is debited & cash account is credited with the purchase amount.
