10. Consider the following equilibrium reaction:

           HSO4−(aq) + OH−(aq) ⇌ SO42−(aq) + H2O(l)

           Which substances are acting as acids in the reaction?

         [A] OH− and SO42−               [B] SO42− and H2O          [C] HSO4− and OH−

         [D] HSO4− and H2O       [E] None of the substances are acting as acids in this reaction

 

     11. The dihydrogenphosphate ion, H2PO4− is amphiprotic. In which of the following reactions is this ion serving as a base?

   I.  H3O+(aq) + H2PO4−(aq) ⇌ H3PO4(aq) + H2O(l)

   II.  H3O+(aq) + HPO42−(aq) ⇌ H2PO4−(aq) + H2O(l)

   III. H3PO4(aq) + HPO42−(aq) ⇌ 2 H2PO4−(aq)

 

  [A] I only     [B] I and II      [C]   I and III      [D] II and III      [E]  I, II and III
