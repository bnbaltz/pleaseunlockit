QUESTION:

How do you find molarity of NaOH based on the data of how many grams of KHP used and how many mL of NaOH used during the titration. 

ANSWER:

Solution:

Given mass of KHP in three trials is used to calculate moles of it.

Then writing reaction between KHP and NaOH, we get mol ratio between KHP and NaOH.

Using mol ratio we can get moles of NaOH. We also know the volume of NaOH in three trial. Volume is converted to Liter and then mole and volume in L are used to find the molarity of NaOH in given three trials.

 

Moles of KHP

Mol = mass in g / molar mass

Molar mass of KHP=204.22 g/mol

Trial

1

n KHP= 0.2547 g / 204.22 g per mol = 0.001247 mol KHP

2

n KHP=0.2202 g / 204.22 g per mol = 0.001078 mol KHP

3

n KHP = 0.2563 g / 204.22 g/mol =0.001255 mol KHP

 

Balanced reaction between NaOH and KHP

 NaOH(aq) +KHC8H4O4 (aq) -- > H2O(l) + KNaC8H4O4(aq)

The balanced reaction gives 1:1 mol ratio between KHP and NaOH.

Therefore, moles of NaOH = moles of KHP

Concentration of NaOH = mol / Volume in L

Concentration of NaOH in three trial

Trial 1

[NaOH]= 0.001247 mol / 0.01263 L = 0.098748 M

Trial 2

[NaOH]= 0.001078 mol / 0.01096 L = 0.09838 M

Trial 3

[NaOH]=0.001255 mol /0.01284 = 0.097743 M
