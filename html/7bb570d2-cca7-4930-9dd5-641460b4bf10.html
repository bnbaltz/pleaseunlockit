Balance sheet:

Balance sheet stands for the statement and information that shows the assets and liabilities of the firm and represents the capital of the firm at a specific period of time. This is used as a detail that shows the balance of firm's income and expenditure for the past year's business performance. Balance sheet represents the financial situation of a firm providing for the summary of the firm's financial balances. Balance sheet shows the firm's total assets and the financing of these assets either through debt or equity.

Current Liabilities:

Liabilities represent the amount owed to others. Liabilities may be the result of past transactions, present obligations, and future payment of assets or services. Current liabilities are called short term liabilities usually have the obligation due within one year or the company’s operating cycle, whichever is longer.

Liabilities:

These are the obligations of the company towards the purchase of merchandize, lending of resources, finance charges etc. These are debt to the company and will be classified as current and non-current liabilities based on the repayment period.

Notes Payable:

It is the note issued by a borrower promising the repayment that will be made along with a predetermined rate of interest. The amount of interest expense charged on note payable will be recorded as expense on income statement.
