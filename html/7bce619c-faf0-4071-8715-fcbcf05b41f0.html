Deuterostome” belongs to the Animalia kingdom. Deuterostomes are bilaterally symmetrical and during embryonic development, the blastopore which is the first opening during cleavage developed into the anus. Echinodermata and Chordata are deuterostomes. All the deuterostomes are triploblastic and have a true coelom.

Lophotrochozoa is protostomes (blastopore developed into a mouth) includes the mollusks, segmented worms, lophophorates, and several other smaller phyla. They have trochophore larvae and lophophore (a feeding structure)

Platyhelminths have no body cavity, they are acoelomates.

Syndermata is pseudocoelomate means fluid-filled false body cavity (hemocoel and reduced coelom).

Ectoprocta has true coelom, their body cavity is surrounded by peritoneum but no schizocoel or enterocoel is formed. 

Brachiopoda has true coelom which is either schizocoelic or enterocoelic. 

Molluscans have hemocoel (fluid-filled body cavity). coelomic fluid and blood circulate through the body cavity (hemocoel) and most of the internal organs baths in it.

Annelida/segmented worms are bilaterally symmetrical have true coelom (schizocoelome). 

Ecdysozoa is protostomes include Nematoda and Orthopoda. The animals that belong to this group can shed their exoskeleton multiple times in their life.

Nematodes are pseudocoelomate i,e HC (hemocoel and reduced coelom).

Arthropods have an open circulatory system so they have hemocoel as in molluscans.
