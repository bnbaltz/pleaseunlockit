QUESTION:

After a skiing accident, your leg is in a cast and supported in a traction device.

Find the magnitude of the force F exerted by the leg on the small pulley. (By Newton's third law, the small pulley exerts an equal and opposite force on the leg.) Let the mass m be 2.50 kg .

T1= 30.0 degrees
T2=30.0 degrees

ANSWER:

 GIVEN:
Mass  ( m ) = 2.50 kg
Tension ( T1  ) = ( T2  ) = T
...........................................................................................
PICTURE:
.........................................................................................................................................
SOLUTION
 
 Tension  T1= T2 = T = mg
 Therefore   T =  2.5 kg x 9.8 m/s2 = 24.5 N
From the figure total force acting   ( F ) = T1 cos 30o  + T2 cos 30o
                                                            = 2 T Cos 30o
                                                             = 2 x 24.5 N x 0.8660
              
                                                             = 42.43 N
