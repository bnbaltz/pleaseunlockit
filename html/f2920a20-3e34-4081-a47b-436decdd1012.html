As given in question a brown mouse and albino mouse produces offspring in ratio 3:1:4 of brown, tan , albino. 

The mode of inheritance of coat color is ---------- option B, epistasis , albino/non-albino  gene epistatic to brown/ tan. 

It is because the ratio obtained is 3:1:4. 

So can't be incomplete Dominance as in it an  intermediate color is expressed in 1:2:1 ratio which is not here. 

Can't be complete Dominance as if brown is Dominant to albino then as per Mendel the ratio can't be 3:1:4. 

The case is epistatic and specifically Recessive epistasis in which a Homozygous Recessive condition of one gene masks the effect of other gene. 

Albino/non-albino is epistatic gene to brown/tan. When albino/non-albino gene is in Dominant condition (non-albino) then it allow to express brown and tan color. But when present in Recessive produces albino. 

So, correct answer is Option B.
