QUESTION:

A bar magnet is pushed through a flat coil of wire. The induced emf is greatest when a.) the north pole is pushed through first b.) the magnet is pushed through quickly c.) the magnet is pushed through slowly d.) the south pole is pushed through first e.) the magnet rests in the coil

ANSWER:

According to Faraday's Law the induced emf in a circuit is equal to the negative of time rate of change of the magnetic flux through the circuit

,  where  is induced emf and  is magnetic flux passing through the circuit.

Therefore the induced emf will be more when the magnet bar is moved quickly as rate of changing of magnetic flux through the coil would be more. And no emf will be induced when the magnet bar rests on coil. The induced emf will not depend upon where the north pole or south pole is pushed first because in both the cases the no of magnetic field lines crossing through the coil will be same.

Hence option (b) is correct, because the magnet is pushed quickly through the coil.
