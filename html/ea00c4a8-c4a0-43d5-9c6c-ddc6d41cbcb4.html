Glassy, Fine grained, Intergrown crystals (crystalline), Bioclastic, Vesicular, Coarse grained, Clastic (gravely, sandy, silt/clay), Layered (folded)

Layered (bedding), Layered (foliated)

ANSWER:

1. This rock specimen is highly deformed in nature. It is layered(folded) in nature. As it suffers deformation, so the the rock may be metamorphic.

2. This specimen shows accumulations of different biogenic shells like bivalve or fragments of shell of different organisms. So, the rock may be sedimentary rock and the rock is bioclastic in nature.

3. The specimen is fine grained in nature. There are vesicles of smaller and larger dimensions are found. Thus the structure is vesicular structure and the rock may be igneous rock.

4. The specimen is coarse grained in nature. Larger grain of quartz and feldspart are found. The rock may be igneous rock. Intergrown crystals are found.

5. This specimen  shows the occurrences of coarse grained minerals. The rock may be igneous.

6. The specimen is glassy in nature. This may be lava. So, the rock is igneous rock.

7. There are clasts of sand grains are found. The specimen may be clastic sedimentary(sandy) rock.

8. There are medium to fine grained minerals found in the specimen. The specimen may be igneous rock as the mineral grains shows interlocking texture.

9. There are association of very large to medium grained minerals in the specimen floated on the fine grained groundmass. There are some euhedral grains are also present. This rock may be igneous rock.

10. This is a fine grained rock. The specimen shows slaty cleavage. The specimen may be fine grained metamorphic rock.

11. This rock is foliated in nature. The rock may be coarse grained metamorphic rock.

12. There are several larger sized grains are present in the specimen. The rock is clastic(gravely) in natute. The rock may be sedimentary rock.
