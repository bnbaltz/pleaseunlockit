QUESTION:

Which of the following is generally true for enzymes, but not traditional chemical catalysts?

They are highly specific to the reaction being catalyzed
   

They do not effect the reaction equilibrium, but lower the reaction's activation energy  
   

They are regulated only by substrate concentration
   

They are recycled at the end of the reaction
   

They are mostly operative under mild temperature, pressure, and pH conditions

ANSWER:

They are highly specific to the reaction being catalyzed

As opposed to chemical catalysts, enzymes are highly specific This specificity is due to the shape of the substrate-binding pocket. This region of the protein only stabilizes a specific substrate due to the non-covalent interactions the pocket makes with groups within the substrate. General chemical catalysts do not have this kind of specificity and rely on chance interactions with the reactants to catalyze reactions.
