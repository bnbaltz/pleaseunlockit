Question 9. 9. (TCO D) On December 31, 2010, Irey Co. has $2,000,000 of short-term notes payable due on February 14, 2011. On January 10, 2011, Irey arranged a line of credit with County Bank that allows Irey to borrow up to $1,500,000 at 1% above the prime rate for 3 years. On February 2, 2011, Irey borrowed $1,200,000 from County Bank and used $500,000 additional cash to liquidate $1,700,000 of the short-term notes payable. The amount of the short-term notes payable that should be reported as current liabilities on the December 31, 2010 balance sheet issued on March 5, 2011 is (Points : 5)

       $0.
       $300,000.
       $500,000.
       $800,000.
 

 

Question 10. 10. (TCO D) Vargas Company has 35 employees who work 8-hour days and are paid hourly. On January 1, 2009, the company began a program of granting its employees 10 days of paid vacation each year. Vacation days earned in 2009 may first be taken on January 1, 2010. Information relative to these employees is as follows:

                 Hourly               Vacation Days Earned       Vacation Days Used
Year          Wages              by Each Employee             by Each Employee
 
2009     $25.80                          10                                 0
2010     27.00                            10                                 8
2011     28.50                            10                                 10

Vargas has chosen to accrue the liability for compensated absences at the current rates of pay in effect when the compensated time is earned. What is the amount of the accrued liability for compensated absences that should be reported at December 31, 2011? (Points : 5)

       $94,920
       $90,720
       $79,800
       $95,760
 

ANSWER:

Answer 6.6

Correct answer is Federal income taxes are not included in an employer's payroll tax expense. Rest all are the payroll related expenses which an employer will have to bear appart from the payroll.

Answer 7.7

Correct answer is State unemployment taxes does not represent a payroll deduction a company may incur.

Answer 8.8

Correct answer is Offsetting current liabilities against assets that are to be applied to their liquidation is not acceptable treatment for the presentation of current liabilities.

Answer 9.9

Correct answer is $300,000 as the initial amount was 2,000,000 which is due on Feb 14, 2011. But on feb 2, 2011 $1,200,000 was borrowed to liquidate the short term notes payable with additional cash of $500,000 making total amount to be liquidated to $1,700,000. Rest 300,000 was due on Feb 14 ,2011 but has not been paid till March 5th 2011. So the amount of $300,000 remains outstanding on the balance sheet and should be reported under current liabilites as of March 5, 2011.

At the end of 2011 the cumulative balance of vacation left per employee = 12 days

So the total amount of accrued vacation at end of year 2011 = cumulative balance of vacation left per employee x Number of hours x Rate per hour x number of employees = 12 x 8 x 28.5 x 35 = $95,760. So the correct answer is $95,760.
