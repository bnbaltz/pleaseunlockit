QUESTION:

1. Which part of the protein synthesis process is affected by Diamond Blackfan Anemia? How do you know?

2. How does Diamond Blackfan Anemia affect the synthesis of proteins? Be as specific as you can.

3. Suppose a mutation was found in a gene that codes for the structure of RNA polymerase. Recall that RNA polymerase is an enzyme responsible for copying DNA sequences into RNA sequences. Predict how this will affect the processes of transcription and translation. Justify your prediction.

ANSWER:

1 Both identified DBA mutated genes encode ribosomal proteins associated with either the small subunit (RPS) or the large subunit (RPL) and the disorder is caused by ribosomal protein haploinsufficiency in these cases.Both identified DBA mutated genes encode ribosomal proteins associated with either the small subunit (RPS) or the large subunit (RPL) and the disorder is caused by ribosomal protein haploinsufficiency in these cases.

Disease heterogeneity can all refer to the number of genes affected, their possible interactions with the environment and modifier genes, and the multitude of potential signalling pathways connecting abortive ribosome synthesis to cell cycle regulators.Relationships between genotype and phenotype occurring over the past year promise to shed light on these dynamic interrelationships and their role in the pathophysiology of DBA.

2 Loss of FLVCR1 induces increased levels of intracellular heme during a process of erythroid differentiation in which synthesis of globin chains has not yet begun. RPS19's haploinsufficiency contributes to impaired biogenesis of the ribosome and thus protein synthesis.

3 Proteins can not be manufactured or poorly produced. If mutations occur in gametes, the DNA of the offspring will be positively, negatively, or neutrally affected.Total modification of the amino acid sequence of a protein is the result of a frameshift mutation. This transition occurs during translation since, in terms of codons, or groups of three nucleotides, ribosomes read the mRNA strand. The reading frame is named for these classes.The key transcription enzyme is RNA polymerase. Transcription starts when the RNA polymerase binds near the beginning of a gene to a promoter sequence (directly or through helper proteins). One of the DNA strands (the template strand) is used by RNA polymerase as a template to create a new, complementary RNA molecule.Mutations of RNA polymerase may increase (rpoB268) or decrease (rpoB255), rpoC3, rpoB265) termination at the IS2 terminator by the rho15 factor. Irrespective of the IS2 insertion or the rho15 mutation, the rpoC1 mutation improves the gal operon transcription.
