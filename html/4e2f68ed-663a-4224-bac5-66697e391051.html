Four major types of global staffing practices are:

a) Ethnocentric. In this strategy, companies retain strong control over foreign subsidiaries by sending expatriates to staff higher positions.

b) Polycentric. In this strategy, companies use home country nationals to run foreign subsidiaries.

c) Regiocentric. In this strategy, focus is on regional strategies which are run by regional employees.

d) Geocentric. In this strategy, focus is on global strategy executed by employees from any location.

The impact of staffing practice on organizational talent has been explained below using a table.

Global Orientation

Impact of Orientation on Staffing

Ethnocentric

Tight control of foreign subsidiaries as key positions held by expatriates

Headquarters country personnel manage all operations.

Home staffing policies replicated in countries.

Talent skills transfer essentially one-way.

Polycentric

Foreign operation treated as independent; local country nationals manage locally; few promotions to HQ.

HCNs have little impact on other countries.

Staffing policies unique to each country.

Little cross-border movement of talent and skills

Regiocentric

Operations managed regionally; communication and coordination high within the region, less so between regions.

Employees circulate within regions with focus on regional business results.

Staffing policies developed and coordinated within regions.

Talent and skills developed within regions, with minimal inter-region transfer.

Geocentric

Organization seen as single international business; management talent comes from any location; strategic plan global in orientation.

Employees circulate throughout global organization.

Staffing policies maximize long-term strength of global organization

Talent and skills deployed globally to achieve global business goals while meeting local requirements.
