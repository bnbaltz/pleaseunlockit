Straight Line Depreciation

Straight Line Depreciation is referred as the method of depreciation where the carrying amount of a long-term asset is distributed even over its life. It is not dependent on the manner in which the asset will be used by an entity. It is the easiest method of depreciation and is highly recommended.

Double-declining Balance Depreciation

It is accelerated depreciation because when compared to straight line method, the depreciation is higher in the initial years and then slows down in the later years. But, the total depreciation expense is the same.

Activity Based Depreciation

It is calculated on the basis of usage of the asset during an accounting period. In other words, it focuses on the usage of machine and not the age of machine. However, it is difficult to estimate the usage of time over a period.
