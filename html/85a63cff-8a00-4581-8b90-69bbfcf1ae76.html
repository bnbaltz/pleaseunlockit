FigureA4.2.1- It is pumice. A type of igneous rocks. This is actually an extrusive volcanic rock. It contains high amount of volatiles and water. After extrusion it loses water and volatiles and after cooling it therefore become very light. It floats on the water. Used as pumice stone also . 

Figure A4.2.2 it is granite. It is also an igneous rock. It is an intrusive rock. It is a plutonic rock. It is rich is Quartz 60% and feldspar20%. Called also as a acidic , leucocratic rock. This granite is a medium to coarse grained rock type. The white coloured part is mostly quartz and feldspar . It also contains associated minerals which are the black coloured one like Biotite or Hornblende.
