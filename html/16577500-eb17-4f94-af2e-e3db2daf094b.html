QUESTION:

A long solenoid with 11.9 turns/cm and a radius of 5.76 cm carries a current of 18.2 mA. A current of 4.77 A exists in a straight conductor located along the central axis of the solenoid. (a) At what radial distance from the axis in centimeters will the direction of the resulting magnetic field be at 47.8° to the axial direction? (b) What is the magnitude of the magnetic field there?

ANSWER:

B inside the solenoid is μ0nI where n is number of turns per meter.

Bsolenoid = (4π*10-7 N/A2)(1190 m-1)(0.0182 A) = 2.722 * 10-5 T, directed along the axis.

B from the wire is μ0I/2πs where s is distance from wire. It will be directed radially around the wire, perpendicular to the solenoid axis.

In order for the total field to have an angle of 47.8 degrees, tan(Bwire/Bsolenoid) = tan 47.8 degrees.

Bwire/Bsolenoid  = 1.1028 thus Bwire  = 3.0015 * 10-5 T

s = μ0I/2πB = (4π*10-7 N/A2)(4.77 A)/(2π)(3.0015 * 10-5 T) = 0.0318 m or 3.18 cm, our first result.

The second result, the magnitude of the field, is just the square root of the sum of the squares of Bwire and Bsolenoid or 4.052 * 10-5 T.
