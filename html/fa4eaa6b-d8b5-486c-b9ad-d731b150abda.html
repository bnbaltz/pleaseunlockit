NOX emissions after 50,000 or fewer miles of driving for one car model vary Normally with mean 0.02 g/mi and standard deviation 0.01 g/mi.

(a) What is the probability that a single car of this model emits more than 0.05 g/mi of NOX? (Round your answer to four decimal places.)

ANSWER:

Light vehicles sold in a country must emit an average of no more than 0.05 grams per mile (g/mi) of nitrogen oxides (NOX) after 50,000 or fewer miles of driving. NOX emissions after 50,000 or fewer miles of driving for one car model vary Normally with mean 0.02 g/mi and standard deviation 0.01 g/mi.

  1. What is the probability that a single car of this model emits more than 0.05 g/mi of NOX? (Round your answer to four decimal places.) 

 

 

Z value for 0.05, z=(0.05-0.02)/0.01 =3

P( x >0.05) = P( z >3)

= 0.0013
 

(b) A company has 49 cars of this model in its fleet. What is the probability that the average NOX level x of these cars is above 0.05 g/mi? (Round your answer to four decimal places.) 

 

Standard error = 0.01/sqrt(49) =0.0014

Z value for 0.05, z=(0.05-0.02)/0.0014 =21.43

P(mean x >0.05) = P( z >21.43)

= 0.0000

A company has 36 cars of this model in its fleet. What is the level L such that the probability that the average NOX level x for the fleet is greater than L is only 0.01?
