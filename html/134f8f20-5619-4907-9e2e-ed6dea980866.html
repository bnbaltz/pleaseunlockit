Part A

Calculate the minimum possible value of the energy E. This lowest possible energy, which is not zero, is called the zero-point energy.

Express your answer in terms of the variables k, m, and appropriate constant

Part B

Calculate the value of x that gives this minimum E.

Express your answer in terms of the variables k, m, and appropriate constants.

Part C

For the x calculated in part B, what is the ratio of the kinetic to the potential energy of the particle?

Express your answer in terms of the variables k, m, and appropriate constants.
