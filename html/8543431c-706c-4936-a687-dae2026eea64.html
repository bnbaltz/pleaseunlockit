QUESTION:

A child of mass 30. kg slides down a ramp of vertical height 5.0 meters. If she starts from rest at the top and there is no friction. what is her speed at the bottom?

 

ANSWER:

Mass of child at the top of ramp, m=30.0 kg

verticle height of ramp, h=5.0 m

speed of the child at the top is u=0.

speed of the child at the bottom, v=?

let potential energy at bottom is zero. 

so that ,   potential energy at the top of ramp is =mgh.

so applying law conservation of energy at top and bottom of ramp,

we have-           

         kinetic energy of child at bottom + potential energy at bottom = kinetic energy of child at top + potential energy at top

                            1/2 * m* v2 +0 = 1/2*mu2 + mgh

                             1/2*mv2 =1/2*m*02 + mgh 

                             1/2*mv2 = mgh

                                v=sqrroot(2gh)

                              v=sqrroot(2*9.8*5)

                               v=9.899495 m/s.

so speed of child at bottom is 9.899495 m/s.
