QUESTION:

A 19 kg object is acted on by a conservative force given by F = -1.0x - 6.0x2, with F in newtons and x in meters. Take the potential energy associated with the force to be zero when the object is at x = 0. (a) What is the potential energy of the system associated with the force when the object is at x = 2.6 m? (b) If the object has a velocity of 5.0 m/s in the negative direction of the x axis when it is at x = 4.5 m, what is its speed when it passes through the origin? What are the answers to (c) (a) and (d) (b) if the potential energy of the system is taken to be -7.9 J when the object is at x = 0?

 

ANSWER:

The energy from the force is ∫F*dx, resulting in energy of the object of -∫F*dx

PE = ∫(1.0*x + 6.0*x²)*dx = (1.0/2)*x² + (6.0/3*x³) + C

PE = 0 at x = 0 so C = 0

at x = 2.6 m , PE = (1.0/2)*2.6² + (6.0/3*2.6³) + 0=3.38+35.152=38.532 J

similarly,The potential energy of the object at x = 4.5 m is 192.375 J, so the total energy at that point is K.E +P.E

192.375 J + 0.5*m*v² =192.375 J+0.5 *19 kg*(5 m/s)^2=192.37+237.5=429.87 J

. Since the PE at origin is 0, all of this is KE so 0.5*m*v0² = 429.87 J-------> v0 = 6.72 m/s

If PE at x = 0 = -7.9 J, then C = -7.9 and

 

PE = (1.0/2)*x² + (6.0/3*x³) - 7.9

 

at x = 2.8 m , PE = 38.532 - 11 = 30.632 J

 

The answer to b is the same, since the change in potential energy is not affected by the constant.
