ANSWER:

(a) 

  • A relation is said to be in 2NF if it is in 1NF and every non key attribute or non prime  attribute  is fully functionally dependent on the primary key or candidate key of the relation.
  • But in this relation non key or non prime attribute "doors"  is partially functionally dependent on the candidate key or primary key of  the relation .
  • So the above relation is not in 2nd   Normal Form(2NF)

(b)

  •   A relation is said to be 3NF if its in 1 NF as well as in 2NF  and also non key or non prime attributes must not have transitive dependency on the  primary key  so that It will not have insertion, update and deletion anomalies.
  •  In the above relation , Colour, Engine , Doors  are functionally  dependant on Model candidate key attribute  while model is functionally dependant on Vincode
  •  Since Model is functionally  dependent on Vincode ,the non prime attributes (Colour, Engine , Doors are transitively dependent on the primary  key Vincode .
  •  Though colour is dependant on model , 2 different models  may have same colour and is partially dependent on Vincode
  • So the above relation is not in 3rd Normal form (3NF).

 

(c)  The logical relational model will be in 3NF as below

            Car (Vincode ,Model, Colour)   - Vincode is the primary key and Model is the foreign key of the Car Model Entity 

            Car Model ( Model,  Engine, Doors)  - Model is the primary key

Note :  

  • Though  Colour is dependent on model attribute , different models may have same colour and  also partially depend on Vincode. So I moved the attribute to Car Relation. If you want to have colour in the Car model relation, you can move this colour attribute to Car Model Relation.
  • Primary keys are bold and underline
  • Foreign keys are in italics
