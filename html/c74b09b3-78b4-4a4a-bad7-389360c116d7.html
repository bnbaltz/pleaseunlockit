QUESTION:

Some RNA molecules are covalently modified by methylation at the N6 position of adenosine residues. Draw the structure of this modified nucleoside.

 

Would this nucleoside be able to participate in Watson-Crick base pairing? Why or why not?

ANSWER:

The methylation at the N​​​​​​6 position of adenosine is caused by a methyl transferase enzyme that covalently attaches methyl group to nitrogen of adenosine. 

The structure of such methylated adenosine is provided below

This type of adenosine can base pair normally with respective pyrimidine molecule as all the hydrogen bonding atoms are at their respective positions, but experimental observations have shown that this methylation decreases the stability of base pair due to steric hindrance.
