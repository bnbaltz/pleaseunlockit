Backflush Costing is a product costing method usually used in an environment of just in time inventory. It delays the process of costing until the manufacturing of goods is finished.

Journal entry is the system of book keeping; the financial transactions will be recorded in chronological order in a general journal. It is the accounting method to express the effect of financial transaction on accounts.

1.

At the time of booking material inventory purchase, the material inventory is debit and accounts payable account is credit. Both debit and credit are made at actual agreed price.

All direct material inventories has been debited due to the rule that debit what comes in and credit what goes out.

Accounts payable has been credited due to the rule that debit is the receiver and credit is the giver.

When the company incurred Actual labor cost and manufacturing cost then that would be added to conversion cost, conversion costs is debit and accrued payroll and miscellaneous accounts is credit.

Conversion cost has been debited due to the rule that debit all expenses and credit all incomes.

Accrued payroll and miscellaneous accounts has been credited due to the rule that debit what comes in and credit what goes out.

At the time of completion of production, material inventories and conversion costs is transfer to finished goods inventory, then finished goods inventory is debit and material inventories and conversion cost is credit based on the standard costing and number of unit completed.

Finished goods inventory has been debited due to the rule that debit what comes in and credit what goes out.

Material inventories and conversion costs has been credited due to the rule that debit what comes in and credit what goes out.
