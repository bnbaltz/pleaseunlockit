QUESTION:

A particular reaction has ΔH = -115 kJ and ΔS = -167 J/K. 

At 25oC, what is the minimum amount of heat that is lost to the surroundings when this reaction occurs?

Enter your answer to 0 decimal places and in units of kJ. Do not include a positive or negative sign in your answer.

ANSWER:

The minimum amount of heat that is lost to the surroundings is given by gibbs free energy , ie.,

ΔG = ΔH - TΔS

where, ΔG is the gibbs free energy change

ΔH is the enthalpy change and ΔS is the entropy change

ΔG = -115 kJ - (25+273.15) K * (-167) J/K

=> ΔG = -114 kJ + (298.15) * 167 J

=> ΔG = -114 kJ + 49791.05 J

=> ΔG = -114 kJ + 49.791 kJ

=> ΔG = -64 kJ

Thus, the minimum amount of heat that is lost to the surroundings is 64 kJ
