QUESTION:

Case Assignment 2

Unilever in Brazil In this case assignment, you need to answer the following questions:

1. Should Unilever divert money from its premium brands to invest in a lower-margin segment of the market?

2. Unilever already has three detergent brands with distinct positioning. Does it need to develop a new brand with a new value proposition, or reposition its existing brands, or use a brand extension?.

3. What price, product, promotion, and distribution strategy would allow Unilever to deliver value to low-income consumers without cannibalizing its own premium brands too heavily?

Maximum 1000 words

ANSWER:

1) Yes , Unilever should divert money from its premium brands to invest in a lower margin segment of the brand as this segment is untapped to a great extent. This presents opportunity to capture a large market. An establish company like Unilever will be able to produce better products than the small players of the low cost segment. In this growing segment , Unilever can gain the first mover advantage.

2) The repositioning of the premium brands as low cost brands has the risk of unfavourable results. Creating a new brand while entering a new segment would increase the costs. Hence brand extension would be the best bet.  It would be preferable to use a brand extension and support it by extensive promotion.

3) Product- The quality should be better then the similarly priced products from the competitors. It's packaging should be such that it doesn't feel like a second rate product. It should produce effective results.

Price - The price could be a little higher than the existing low cost segment products yet it should be easily affordable and lower than the premium brands.

Promotion-  Platforms of the mass media should be used for promotion.

Distribution- The distribution should be done in the areas where the target customers reside.
