QUESTION:

A cat rides a merry-go-round while turning with uniform circular motion. At time t1 = 2.00 s, the cat's velocity is v1 = (3.50) i + (4.00 m/s) j, measured on a horizontal xy coordinate system. At time t2 = 5.00 s, a half-revolution later, its velocity is v2 = (-3.50 m/s) i + (-4.00 m/s) j.
(a) What is the magnitude of the cat's centripetal acceleration?
m/s2

(b) What is the magnitude of the cat's average acceleration during the time interval t2 - t1?
m/s2

ANSWER:

Given data:
the cat's velocity is v1 = (3.50 m/s) i + (4.00 m/s) j at  t1 = 2.00 s
the cat's velocity is  v2 = (-3.50 m/s) i + (-4.00 m/s) j at t2 = 5.00 s
SOLUTION:
a)
      The magnitude of the cat's velocity is given by
                    v = [(3.50 m/s)2+(4.00 m/s)2]1/2
                       = 5.315 m/s
      The time period for the total revolution of the cat will be
                         T = 2πr/v
      For half revolution,
        (5.00 s - 2.00 s) = πr/(5.315 m/s)
                        3.00 s = πr/(5.315 m/s)
      Therefore, the radius of the circular path will be
                  r = [(3.00 s)(5.315 m/s)]/(π)
                    = 5.08 m
      So, the magnitude of the cat's centripetal acceleration can be calculated as
               a = v2/r
                  = (5.315 m/s)2/(5.08 m)
                  = 5.56 m/s2
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
b)
      The change in velocity is given by
          v2 - v1 = ((-3.50 m/s) i + (-4.00 m/s))-((3.50 m/s) i + (4.00 m/s) j)
                      = (-7.00 m/s)i - (8.00 m/s)j
      The cat's average acceleration during the time interval t2 - t1 will be
                   a = [v2 - v1 ]/[t2 - t1]
                     = [(-7.00 m/s)i - (8.00 m/s)j]/[5.00 s - 2.00 s]
                     = [(-7.00 m/s)i - (8.00 m/s)j]/[3.00 s]
      The magnitude of the cat's average acceleration during the time interval t2 - t1 will be
                 |a| = [[(-7.00 m/s)2+(-8.00 m/s)2]1/2]/(3.00 s)
                     = 3.54 m/s2
