ANSWER:

Question 1.

The quartz sandstone in the microscope will contain two distinct part, viz. Matrix and frameworks. The matrix are more finer parts while the frameworks being the more coarser ones. The matrix tend to occupy the spaces between the frameworks. 

And in case of fine grained quartz sandstone even, the matrix will have cement within it which bounds them. They will surely lack a crystalline texture.

While the quartzite will have a recrystallised texture under the microscope. This mainly differentiates the quartzites from the quartz sandstone.

 

Question 2. Although from a distance a metamorphic outcrop may look like a bedded sedimentary outcrop but when we closely analyze them, we can be able to distinguish them differently.

First we have to break the parts of outcrop to collect some hand specimens where we can closely look to them and identify whether they are metamorphic rocks or sedimentary rock. We can also take sample to the laboratory and analyze them under microscope for a better inference and more confidence.
