QUESTION:

Microwave ovens use microwave radiation to heat food. The microwaves are absorbed by the water molecules in the food, which is transferred to other components of the food. As the water becomes hotter, so does the food. Suppose that the microwave radiation has a wavelength of 122 cm . How many photons are required to heat 262 mL of coffee from 24.4 oC to 59.1 oC? Assume that the coffee has the same density, 0.997 g/mL , and specific heat capacity, 4.184 J/(gâ‹…K) , as water over this temperature range. 

a) 2.33 x10^29

b) 3.45 X10^33

c) 2.46 x10^30

d) 1.57 x10^28

e) 4.57 x10^32

ANSWER:

Step 1: calculate total energy required

Given:

m = density * volume

 = 0.997 g/mL * 262 mL

 = 261.2 g

C = 4.184 J/g.oC

Ti = 24.4 oC

Tf = 59.1 oC

use:

Q = m*C*(Tf-Ti)

Q = 261.2*4.184*(59.1-24.4)

Q = 37922 J

 

Step 2: calculate number of photons

 

 

Given:

lambda = 122 cm = 1.22 m

1st calculate energy of 1 photon

 

use:

E = h*c/lambda

=(6.626*10^-34 J.s)*(3.0*10^8 m/s)/(1.22 m)

= 1.629*10^-25 J

 

number of photon = total energy/energy of 1 photon

n = 37922.0/1.629*10^-25

= 2.33*10^29

 

Answer: a
