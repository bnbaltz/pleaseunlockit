Positive displacement pump: Positive displacement pumps are used to move the fluid by trapping a fixed volume of fluid in a cavity and then forces it into the discharge pipe. During the process, the cavity of the suction side increases and the cavity of the discharge side decreases. When the cavity of the discharge side collapses the fluid flows. The volume of liquid is SAME for each cycle of this process. 

There are two broad classes of Positive Displacement pumps:

1) Reciprocatory pump: These types of pumps use the reciprocatory motion to pump fluid. Eg: Plunger pump. In plunger pump,  there is a cylinder with a reciprocating plunger. In the suction process, the plunger retracts and due to suction, the liquid comes inside the cylinder. In the forward motion, the plunger pushes the fluid out of the cylinder. 

2) Rotatory Pumps: These types of pumps use the rotatory motion to pump fluid. Eg: Gear pumps.              In gear pump, the liquid is trapped by the gear teeth in the opening between two identical gears and with the help of pump. On the other side the liquid is squeezed out when teeth of two gears are rotated against each other.
