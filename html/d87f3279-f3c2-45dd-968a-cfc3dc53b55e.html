Warranties

Any products buy by customer may ask for warranty of the product so that he can rely on the products. Warranties are the examples of contingent liability. There are some terms and conditions covered to put product in warranties. If after sale this kind of problem arises then product will be replaced by company without any further cost.

In the given case Computer Wholesalers restores and resells notebook computers. Company acquires notebook from corporations to upgrade computer system for warranty of 90 days. Past experience says company warranty cost to 6% of sales and sales for the month of December is $600,000. Actual expenditure in January is $13,000.
