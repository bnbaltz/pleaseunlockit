QUESTION:

A sample of 137Cs (half life 30.1 years) decays at a rate of 1010 decays per second. How long will it take for the decay rate to decrease to 107 decays per second?

ANSWER:

No = 1010 decays/second
N = 107 decays/second

half life = 30.1 years
k = 0.693/half life
 = 0.693/(30.1)
 = 0.0230 years-1

use 1st order decay equation:
N = No*e^(-k*t)
107 = 1010 *e^(-0.0230*t)
e^(-0.0230*t) =0.10594
0.0230*t  = 2.2449
t = 97.6 years

Answer:  97.6 years
