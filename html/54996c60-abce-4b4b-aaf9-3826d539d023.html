Question 26:

Artificial insemination is a simpler process than in-vitro fertilization (IVF).

In the process of artificial insemination, the semen is directly introduced into the womb of a woman. The fertilization takes place in the uterus of the woman while in IVF, the sperm and the egg are fused together in a laboratory dish.

Hence, the fourth option is correct.

In IVF, a woman is given additional treatment with FSH and LH to inside superovulation, but in artificial insemination (also known as intrauterine insemination (IUI)) also FSH and LH are given to the woman to stimulate the ovaries to produce more than one follicle, hence increasing the chance of fertilization. 

During IVF, the sperm and the egg (gametes) are fused together in a laboratory dish and the fertilized zygote is introduced into the female. In artificial insemination, the semen is introduced into the woman's womb in hopes of increasing the chances of fertilization.

Both techniques of IVF and artificial insemination can be used for surrogacy.

Thus, option four is the correct answer.
