Equity-method investment

When one company holds 20% - 50% shares of another company, it is termed as Equity Method Investment.

Under the equity method investment, the investor should account for net income from the investee as an increase in the equity investment account and equity income. Hence,

Company S’s reported net income of $20 million will increase Company WD’s equity investments and equity income by $8 million.
