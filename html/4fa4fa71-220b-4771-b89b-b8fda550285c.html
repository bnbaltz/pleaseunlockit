The well graded soils are the soils in which there is a wide variation of particle sizes the smaller particles fit into and fill up the most of pores between the larger particles. Or the soils which comprises of all particle sizes are called well graded soils.

The poor graded soils are the soils which consist of the soil grains in a very narrow range of sizes. Or the soils in which some particle sizes are omitted or not present are called as the poor graded soils.

The well graded soils have low porosity. The poor graded soils have high porosity.

The well graded soils have low permeability. The poor graded soils have high permeability.

The A represents well graded soils which has low porosity and low permeability.

The B represent Poor graded soils which has high porosity and high permeability.

If the uniform conformity is 50 for a soil than it is considered as the well graded soil.
