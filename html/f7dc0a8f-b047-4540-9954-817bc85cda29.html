QUESTION:

ABCD is a square. The circle with center A and radius AB meets the perpendicular bisector of AD is two points , one of which O is the one inside the square. Find the measure of angle AOC.

Please provide an explanation to how I may approach this problem.

ANSWER:

Here are some hints: line segment AY is half of radius AO. Do you know of a special right triangle that has sides like this? If you do, you already know angle AOY.

Draw in line segment BO. What can you say about triangle OAB (two equal sides)? Angle OAB is 90 - angle YAO.

Angle BOA is 1/2 (180-OAB). Another way to find angle OAB is to note that it is an alternating interior angle with angle YOA.

Once you have angle BOA, add it to angle YOA and subtract the sum from 180 to get angle BOZ.

Segment BZ = ZC (definition of bisector)

Segment OZ = OZ (reflexive)

<BZO = <CZO (all perpendicular angles are equal)

So triangle BZO = CZO (SAS)

So <BOZ = COZ (corresponding parts of congruent triangles are congruent)

YOC = 180 - COZ

and then add <AOY to <COZ to get <AOC
