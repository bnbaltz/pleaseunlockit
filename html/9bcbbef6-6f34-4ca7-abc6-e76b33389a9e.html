QUESTION:

A stationary gas turbine plant operates on the Brayton cycle and delivers 20,000 kW to an electric generator. The maximum temperature is 1200 K and the minimum temperature is 290 K. The minimum and maximum pressures are 95 and 380 kPa, respectively. Calculate :
a-
The power output of the turbine.
b- The fraction of the output of the turbine which is used to drive the compressor.
c- The mass and volume flow rates at inlet to the compressor.

ANSWER:

W out = 20,000 KW

 

T max = 1200 K = T3

 

T min = 290 K = T1

 

P2 = P3 = 380 KPa

 

P1 = P4 = 95 Kpa

 

T2/T1 = (P2/P1) ^ (k-1/k)

 

k = 1.4

 

∴ T2/290 = (380/95) ^ (1.4 -1 / 1.4) 

T2 = 430.94 K

T2/T1 = T3/T4 =

430.94/290 = 1200/T4

T4 = 807.54 K

 

a) Power output of turbine = Cp.(T3 - T4)

       Wt = 1.005 x (1200 - 807.54) = 394.422 KJ/Kg

b) Compressor work = Cp. (T2 - T1)

 Wp = 1.005 x (430.94 - 290) = 141.64 KJ/Kg

∴ Percentage  used = Wp/Wt = 141.64 / 394.422 = 0.359

Hence 35.9 % of turbine is used in pump

c)  Net work = Wt - Wp = 394.422 - 141.64 = 252.78 Kj/Kg 

∴ m x 252.78 = 20,000

∴ mass flow rate  = 79.11 kg/s

 

volume flow rate at inlet to compressor =

P.V. = m.Rair x T

∴ 95 x10^3 x V = 79.11 x 287.05 x 290

V = 69.32 m^3/Kg
