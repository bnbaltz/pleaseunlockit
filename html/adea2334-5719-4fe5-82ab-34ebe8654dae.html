QUESTION:

The potential difference across the ends of a wire is doubled in

magnitude. If Ohm’s law is obeyed, which one of the following statements

concerning the resistance of the wire is true? EXPLAIN WHY???

A---The resistance is not changed

B--The resistance is twice its original value

C---The resistance increases by a factor of four

D---The resistance is one half of its original value

ANSWER:

Resistance of a body(of fixed shape and size) is dependent only on temperature.
R=(ρ*l/a)

Ohm's law states that V=IR

If you double the voltage current will be doubled. Resistance remains same.

Option A
