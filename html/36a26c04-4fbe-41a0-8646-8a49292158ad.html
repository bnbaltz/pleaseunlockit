QUESTION:

A 5.00 kg rock is dropped and allowed to fall freely. Find the initial kinetic energy, the final kinetic energy, and the change in kinetic energy for a) the first 2.00 m of the fall and b) the second 2.00 m of the fall.

ANSWER:

KE = (1/2)mv2

Initial kinetic energy is zero because it hasn't started moving yet.  There are a couple of ways to get the later kinetic energy but using potential energy is by far the easiest. 

Gravitation potential energy is mgh.  Higher heights have higher potential energies, which as the object descends gets converted to kinetic energy.  

In two meters, this object loses (5.00 kg)(9.81 m/s2)(2.0 m) = 98.1 J of potential energy and gains that much kinetic. 

so:  first two meters of fall, starting KE 0, ending KE 98.1 J, gain of 98.1 J

second two meters of fall, starting KE 98.1 J, ending KE 196.2 J, gain of 98.1 J
