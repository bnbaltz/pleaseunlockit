QUESTION:

How has Unilever targeted the black population in Brazil? How does the company’s strategy in Brazil differ from its strategy in other countries? What does your response tell you about Unilever’s overall global marketing strategy?

ANSWER:

Unilever, in Brazil, initially marketed its products by targeting it at Black people in the similar fashion that proved effective for the company in USA. But this marketing effort of Unilever was shunned by the Black Brazilians. Unilever had ignored the basic fact that Black Brazilians were socioculturally different from the American Blacks.

Unilever later marketed its products in Brazil after acknowledging the differences. For instance in Brazil (compared to America) Blacks were better integrated into the society, were not as discriminated as in USA and racial self identification was not very strong.

Thus Unilever targeted the black population in Brazil by targeting the entire population rather than certain segments. This was due to the fact that Brazil’s blacks do not think of themselves as being simply black but as falling into a range of skin tones.

In Brazil the company is using a localization approach in marketing. Its advertisements show people with different skin tones and not just blacks or whites. Globally, due to increased level of competition the company uses a global standardization strategy.
