QUESTION:

Hawaiian Specialty Foods purchased equipment for $27,000. Residual value at the end of an estimated four-year service life is expected to be $2,700. The machine operated for 2,800 hours in the first year, and the company expects the machine to operate for a total of 15,000 hours.

 

Calculate depreciation expense for the first year using each of the following depreciation methods: (1) straight-line, (2) double-declining-balance, and (3) activity-based. (Do not round your intermediate calculations.)

 

Stright line -

Double declining balance -

ANSWER:

Question 1

Particulars   Amount
Depreciation Expense for First Year as per    
Straight Line Method    6,075
Double Declining Balance Method   13,500
Activity Based     4,536

 

 

Part 1

Depreciation Expense using Straight Line Method

Depreciation = Cost of Equipment - Salvage Value of Equipment / Useful Life of Equipment in Years

Cost of Equipment = $ 27,000

Salvage Value of Equipment = $ 2,700

Useful Life of Equipment in Years = 4 Years

Depreciation = 27,000 - 2,700 / 4

Annual Depreciation = $ 6,075

 

Depreciation Expense on Equipment 1 as per Straight Line Method = $ 6,075

 

Part 2

Depreciation Expense using Double Declining Balance Method

Depreciation = Cost of Equipment - Salvage Value of Equipment / Useful Life of Equipment in Years

Cost of Equipment = $ 27,000

Salvage Value of Equipment = $ 2,700

Useful Life of Equipment in Years = 4 Years

Depreciation = 27,000 - 2,700 / 4

Annual Depreciation = $ 6,075

 

Depreciation Rate as per Double Declining Balance Method = Annual Depreciation / (Cost of Equipment - Salvage Value of Equipment) * 200%

Depreciation Rate as per Double Declining Balance Method = 6,075 / (27,000 - 2,700) * 200%

Depreciation Rate as per Double Declining Balance Method = 50%

 

Depreciation for Year 1 as per Double Declining Balance Method = Cost of Asset * Depreciation Rate as per Double Declining Balance Method

Cost of Equipment = $ 27,000

Depreciation Rate as per Double Declining Balance Method = 50%

Depreciation for Year 1 as per Double Declining Balance Method = 27,000 * 50%

Depreciation for Year 1 as per Double Declining Balance Method = $ 13,500

 

 

Part C

Depreciation Expense using Activity Based 

Depreciation = Cost of Equipment - Salvage Value of Equipment / Useful Life of Equipment in Hours

Cost of Equipment = $ 27,000

Salvage Value of Equipment = $ 2,700

Useful Life of Equipment in Hours = 15,000 Hours

Depreciation = 27,000 - 2,700 / 15000

Annual Depreciation Rate per Hour = $ 1.62 per Hour

 

Depreciation Expense for Year 1 = Annual Depreciation Rate per Hour * Actual Hours Used in Year 1

Actual Hours used in Year 1 = 2,800 Hours

Annual Depreciation Rate per Hour = $ 1.62

Depreciation Expense for Year 1 = 2,800 * 1.62

Depreciation Expense for Year 1 = $ 4,536
