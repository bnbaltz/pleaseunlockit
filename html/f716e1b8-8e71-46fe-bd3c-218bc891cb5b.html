QUESTION:

The apparatus consists of a glass sphere that contains an electron gun, which generates a focused beam of electrons. A measured current is applied to the Helmholtz coils so that the magnitude of the magnetic field within the electron tube can be calculated. A measured accelerating potential (V) is then applied to the electron gun. The magnetic field (B) deflects the electron beam in a circular path with a radius (r) that is measured using the illuminated mm scale.

Assume you perform the experiment in the real world and turn off the current in the Helmholtz coils. Our planet earth has a magnetic field. In which direction do you have to point the electron beam so that you do not see a deflection of the beam due to the Earthâ€™s magnetic field?

ANSWER:

The electron should point towards North or south. Then The beam will not be deflected.

Becasue, magnetic force acting on moving charged particle,  

F = q*v*B*sin(theta)

here theta is the angle between v and B.

when electron beam points towards north, theta = 0

so, F = q*v*B*sin(0)

 = 0

when electron beam points towards south, theta = 180

so, F = q*v*B*sin(180)

 = 0
