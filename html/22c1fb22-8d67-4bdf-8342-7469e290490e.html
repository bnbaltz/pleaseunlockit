ANSWER:

1) Hyaline cartilage contains less collagen fibers and it is soft, whereas fibrocartilage contains many fibers and it is stiff. Hyaline cartilage facilitates lubricated and smooth movements of the bones present at joints. Fibrocartilage provides restricted mobility to the joints and attaches bones with other bones. 

2) Lacuna are found between the lamellae.

3) Spongy bone tissue has long narrow lacuna surrounded by long narrow fibers going all in the same direction.

4) Compact bone tissue has lacuna which radiate out giving it a star like appearance. This is due to the actin filaments and this is crucial for the maintenance of shape.

5) When looking at a slide you see what appears to be open spaces with small cells in it, this is all surrounded by layers of connective tissue. this is hyaline connective tissue.
