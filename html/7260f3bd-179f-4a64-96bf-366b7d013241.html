QUESTION:

If a coin is tossed 6 times, and then a standard six-sided die is rolled 2 times, and finally a group of three cards are drawn from a standard deck of 52 cards without replacement, how many different outcomes are possible?

ANSWER:

(i) When a coin is tossed once, we get 2 outcomes (Heads or Tails)

Therefore, when a coin is tossed n times the total outcomes = 2n

(ii) When a dice is tossed once, we get 6 outcomes (1, 2, 3, 4, 5 or 6)

When a dice is tossed n times, total outcomes = 6n

(iii) When a card is picked for the 1st time, there are 52 possible outcomes. For each subsequent pick without replacement, the number of outcomes keeps reducing by 1.

Therefore:

When the coin is tossed 6 times, total outcomes = 26 = 64

Therefore when a dice is tossed twice, the total outcomes = 62 = 36

When we pick 3 cards without replacement (It is a Group), the total outcomes = 52 C3 = 22100

Total possible outcomes = 64 * 36 * 22100 = 5,09,18,400
