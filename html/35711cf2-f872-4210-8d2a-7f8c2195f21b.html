QUESTION:

Which of the following is true for a system at equilibrium? Question 6 options: a) ΔS°sys = ΔS°surr b)ΔS°sys = -ΔS°surr c)ΔS°sys= ΔS°surr = 0 d)ΔS°univ > 0 e) None of these is a sufficient condition.

ANSWER:

b)ΔS°sys = -ΔS°surr 

is the correct option

deltaS universe or total= delta S system- delta S surroundings 

delta S surroundings= -delta H/ T 

Considering that freezing is an exothermic reaction, delta H will be negative causing delta S surroundings to have a positive sign. 

For delta S system, use Hess' law the summation of the entropies of the products subtracted by the summation of the entropies of the reactants. The entropies you need to check are delta S of fusion, which could be found in your textbook, I assume. 

Nevertheless, since water is being frozen, there is a decrease in entropy, making delta S system negative. 

Whichever has a larger value, delta S systems or delta S surroundings will determine the sign for delta S universe
