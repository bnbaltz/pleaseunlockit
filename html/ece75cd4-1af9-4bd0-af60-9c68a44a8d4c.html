QUESTION:

Explain why the pigmentation normally found only in the animal pole is now more widespread?

ANSWER:

Pigmentation :- Pigmentation means coloring. ... Your skin gets its color from a pigment called melanin. Special cells in the skin make melanin. When these cells become damaged or unhealthy, it affects melanin production. Some pigmentation disorders affect just patches of skin.

Pigmentation is used by many animals for protection, by means of camouflage, mimicry, or warning coloration. Pigments such as melanins in the skin may serve to protect tissues from ultraviolet radiation.
