QUESTION:

Consider an electrochemical cell that has a nickel electrode immersed in 1.0 M Ni2+ and a platinum electrode immersed in 0.010 M Co2+.

Co2+ + 2e– --> Co E° = –0.28 V

Ni2+ + 2e– -->  Ni E° = –0.23 V

Calculate the concentration of Ni2+ if the cell is allowed to run to equilibrium at 25°C.

A) 1.0 M B) 1.1 M C) 1.9 x10–4 M D) 0.020 M E) none of these

ANS: D 

ANSWER:

The E°Ni2+/Ni > E°Co2+/Co

Hence, Ni2+ undergoes reduction to Ni, whereas Co undergoes oxidation to Co2+.

Therefore, E°cell = E°Ni2+/Ni - E°Co2+/Co

i.e. E°cell = -0.23 -(-0.28) = 0.05 V

At equilibrium, Ecell = 0

Formula: Ecell = Eocell - (0.0591/n) Log[Co2+]/[Ni2+]

Here, n = 2 and [Co2+]e = 1 M, where 'e' stands for equilibrium.

i.e. 1/[Ni2+] = 100.05/0.02955 = 49.209

i.e. [Ni2+]e = 0.020 M
