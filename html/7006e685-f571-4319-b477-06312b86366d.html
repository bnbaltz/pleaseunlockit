According to statute of frauds, the contracts that involve transfer of an interest in land should be in writing. But there are some exceptions to the writing requirement which include admission, performance and promissory estoppel. Here Lisa has promised to transfer half of the assets to Peter after his divorce. Peter has relied on that promise and paid the property taxes for the estate which resulted into his detriment. According to promissory estoppel, such promises where the promisee relies on the promise to his subsequent detriment are legally binding and do not require to be in writing. Hence this is case holds an exception for the writing requirement in statute of frauds based on promissory estoppel.
