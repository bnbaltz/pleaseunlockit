Direct Costs:

Direct costs are those costs which are easily identifiable with the product or a specific cost center. Direct costs differ with the level of outputs and will be constant for each unit of production.

Examples: Furniture, Plant and machinery and buildings cost.

Indirect Costs:

Indirect costs are those costs which are not easily identifiable with the product or a specific cost center. Indirect costs are usually constant for all levels of outputs.

Examples: Depreciation, insurance and salaries to employees.
