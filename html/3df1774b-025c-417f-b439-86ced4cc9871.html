QUESTION:

Worker productivity is one of the most important factors that helps us determine the performance of an economy. Which of the following best describes productivity?
a. capital per worker b. total number of hours worked c. per capita gdp d. total output in one year e.percentage change in output

ANSWER:

Workers productivity is the most important factor that helps to assess the performance of the economy. Better productivity will increase output of the economy. It will maximize gross domestic product.  For describing productivity consider each point below separately-

a. Capital per worker: It indicates input capital used on an average by each worker. It will not indicate his productivity or effeciency. High capital per worker combined with low output is an indicator of ineffeciency.

b. Like previous one, total number of hours worked is not an indicator of effeciency. X and Y both have worked for 10 hours. But X has produced 10 units while Y has produced 15 units. Here productivity of Y is better.

c. Per capita GDP. It is total GDP of the economy divided by total population.If it gets better then it will indicate improvment in productivity.

d. Total output without comparing with inputs used cannot be judged for productivity.

e. Percentage  change in output may be on the higher side. But if percentage change in inputs used in production is higher then it will indicate low productivity.

Answer: Option c is correct.
