ANSWER:

Let V be a vector space over F and let W be a subspace of V. 

Let q : V to V/W defined by q(v) = v + W

(a)  Let u and v belongs to V then q(u+v) = (u+v) + W = (u+W) + (v+W) = q(u) + q(v) 

Let c be a scalar in F and let v be a vector in V 

Then q(cv) = cv + W = c ( v + W)  [ since W is subspace] = cq(v) 

Therefore q is a linear transformation. 

Also for each U in V/W there is an v in V such that U = v + W = q(v) 

Therefore q is onto. 

Identity of the quotient space V/W is W

Let v belongs to N(q) then q(v) = W implies v + W = W implies v  belongs to W and hence N(q) is subset of W

Again let u belongs to W since W is subspace of V, u belongs to V

So q(u) = u + W = W implies u belongs to N(q) 

Hence W is subset of N(q) 

Therefore N(q) = W

(b) Since q is onto Im(q) = V/W

Since V is finite dimensional W is finite dimensional and so V/W is finite dimensional. 

By Rank Nullity theorem ( Dimension theorem) 

dim Null(q) + dim Im(q) = dim V

or, dim N(q) + dim V/W = dim V

or, dim W + dim V/W = dim V

or, dim V/W = dim V - dim W.
