QUESTION:

Hypernatremia occurs when the sodium concentration in the blood gets too high. Why might this be a concern in patients with extensive third-degree burns?

A. Loss of nerve endings leads to an inability to properly control body temperature.

B. Extensive blistering of the epidermis causes loss of serous fluid.

C. Damage to sweat glands results in uncontrolled sweating and loss of body fluid.

D. Inability to produce vitamin D results in decreased sodium absorption from the digestive system.

E. Disruption of the epidermal water barrier can cause severe dehydration.

ANSWER:

C. Damage to sweat glands results in uncontrolled sweating and loss of body fluid.

Explanation;

Third-degree (full thickness) burns
Third-degree burns damage or completely destroy both layers of skin including hair follicles and sweat glands and damage underlying tissues. These burns always require skin grafts.
Third-degree burns destroy the epidermis and dermis. Third-degree burns may also damage the underlying bones, muscles, and tendons.
