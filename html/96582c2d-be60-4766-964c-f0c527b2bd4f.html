QUESTION:

Anhydrous CuSO4 is white in colour but a solution of CuSO4 turns blue. which of the statements is/are true for this observation.Give reason for each options.

1. It forms a hydrated compound,thus regaining its colour.

2. The colour is due to metal-to-ligand charge transfer transistion.

3, The octahedral aquo-complex gives rise to laporte relaxed d-d transition.

4.   The octahedral aquo-complex gives rise to spin-allowed and laporte-allowed LMCT transition.

5.  The octahedral aquo-complex give  rise to laporte-allowed d-d transition.

Options.

(a.) 1. and 3.

(b.)  2. and 4.

(c)  2.

(d) 5.

(e.) 4.

 

 

ANSWER:

Answer: Anhydrous CuSO​​​​​​4 is white in colour while solution of copper sulphate turns into blue colour.

Statement 1: it forms hydrated compound , thus regaining its colour.

This statement is true because, In hydrated copper sulphate compound the copper(+2) ion is surrounded by four water molecules. For this coordinate covalent bond formation the compound show d-d transition. So that the blue colour appears in the visible region. But in case of anhydrous copper sulphate, there are no water molecules present. So the CFSE value remains zero. And no charge transfer or transition occurs. That's why it is colourless.

this is the octahedral aq complex. Due to Jahn Teller Distortion, two axial bonds willbe longer.

Statement 2: The colour is due to metal to ligand charge transfer: this is wrong statement .the colour appears due to d-d electronic transition. Moreover , for metal to ligand charge transfer the ligand should be electron rich and having low oxidation state(0,1,-1) with e deficient ligand. This requirements are not fulfilled.

Statement 3:  the octahedral aqua complexes gives rise to the Laporte relaxed d-d transition - wrong statement .the complex is Laporte forbidden. 

Statement 4: the octahedral aqua complexes gives rise to the spin allowed and Laporte allowed LMCT transition. This is wrong statement .here no ligand to metal charge transfer occurs. And the  complex is spin allowed but Laporte forbidden.

Statement 5: the octahedral aqua complexes gives rise to the Laporte allowed d-d translation. This is wrong statement.
