Consolidate financial statements of two companies

When a parent company has more than 50% ownership of the voting stock of the subsidiary company, then it is appropriate to consolidate financial statement of both the companies (parent and subsidiary).

Here, Company WD owns 100% of the outstanding stock of Company S. So, Company WD (parent company) would combine its inventory with Company Sâ€™ (subsidiary company) inventory.

Hence, Company WD would report total inventory of $22,000 in the consolidated financial statements.
