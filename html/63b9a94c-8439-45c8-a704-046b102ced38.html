QUESTION:

3 data scientists work on a cluster which has a daily limit of 2 compute ’credits’. When the total daily amount of compute credits of the three data scientists exceeds that daily limit, the company is charged a one thousand dollar penalty. Assume each data scientist’s daily cluster usage in compute credits is distributed between 0 and 1 and that the chance the usage belongs in some subinterval of [0, 1] is equal to the length of the subinterval. Assume also that the daily usages of the data scientists across the year are independent. You are asked to estimate the total cost of this penalty over one business year (252 days) with a single number. What is your succinct response?

ANSWER:

Total usage of compute credits for all 3 scientists in a day would vary between [0,3]. Now, it is clear in the question that it would be equally likely to have total compute credit in that interval at any point. Now across an year, on an average they will cross the limit of 2 compute credits once in three days. Because probability of [2,3] in [0,3] is 1/3.
Hence, total penalty would be (252/3)*1000 = $84000.
