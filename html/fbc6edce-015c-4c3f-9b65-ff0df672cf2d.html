ANSWER:

Testes in male embryo is responsible for producing a hormone called testosterone which is one of the androgens in human body. Mullerian duct system present in the embryo forms the female reproductive parts i.e. cervix,uterus,oviducts and upper vagina.  testes secrete Anti Mullerian Hormone(AMH). This hormone will gradually destroy and make the Mullerian duct system disapper the production of testosterone by the testes protect the Wolfian duct system. Wolfian duct system goes on to become the male reproductive system consisting of epidydimis,seminal vesciles, vas deferens. 

1. In absence of testes which indicate AMH will not be secreted and hence the MULLERIAN duct system will not     disapper and lack of testosterone will mean that the male duct system will not be able to develop. therefore the embryo will develop female sex accessory organs.

2.In an intact male embryo when antiandrogen is given it will block the effect of testosterone which will impair the WOLFIAN duct system development(male reproductive system) and testes will continue the release of AMH which will cause the MULLERIAN duct (female reproductive system)to disapper and hence neither duct system develop
