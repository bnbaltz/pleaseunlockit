QUESTION:

a.) A box contains 15 marbles: 5 blue, 5 red, and 5 green. If three marbles are removed, one at a time, without replacement, what is the probability that first a red is removed, then two greens?

 

b.) A box contains 15 marbles: 5 blue, 5 red, and 5 green. If three marbles are removed, one at a time, without replacement, what is the probability that first a blue is removed, then a red is removed, then a green is removed?

ANSWER:

a) There are 15 marbles in total out of which 5 are red, 5 are blue and 5 are green marbles. The probability that first a red is removed, then two greens = (5/15) * (5/14) * (4/13) = 0.0366. (Ans).

b) The probability that first a blue marble is removed, then a red is removed and then a green = (5/15) * (5/14) * (5/13) = 0.0458. (Ans).
