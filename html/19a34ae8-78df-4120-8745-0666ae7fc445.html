QUESTION:

What are coordinates of the center of mass for the uniform plate shown in the figure below? (x1 = 19 cm, y1 = 16 cm, and y2 = 3 cm. The figure is not shown to scale.)

ANSWER:

find the mass and center of mass of each piece of plate in eachquadrant. The mass is directly proportional to the area (since itis a uniform plate); so we will just find the area.

in the first quadrant (top right)
    center of mass is:
          CM1 =(x1/2, y1 - y2/2) = (19cm/2, 16cm- 3cm/2)
                 = (9.5, 14.5)cm
    area is: A1 = 3cm * 19cm =57 cm2

in the second quadrant (top left)
    center of mass is:
          CM2 =(-10cm/2, y1/2)
                = (-5, 8)cm
    area is: A2 = 10cm * 16cm =160cm2

in the third quadrant (bottom left)
    center of mass is:
          CM3 =(-10cm/2, -20cm/2)
                = (-5, -10)cm
          area is: A3= 20cm * 10cm = 200cm2

in the fourth quadrant (bottom right)
    center of mass is:
          CM4 =(10cm/2, -10cm - 10cm/2)
                = (5, -15)cm
          area is: A4= 10cm * 10cm = 100cm2

the center of mass of the plate is then the weighted sum of eachindividual center of mass, divided by the total mass (area)

the x component is
    X = (9.5 * 57 + -5 * 160 + -5 * 200 + 5 * 100)/ (57 + 160 + 200 + 100)
       = -1.467 cm

the y component is:
    Y = (14.5 * 57 + 8 * 160 + -10 * 200 + -15 *100) / (57 + 160 + 200 + 100)
       = -2.695 cm
