QUESTION:

Tyler sells bees and beekeeping supplies. Bees (including a queen) are shipped in special packages according to weight. The target weight of a package is 1.4 kg. Historically, Tylerâ€™s shipments have weighed on average 1.4 kg, with a standard deviation of 0.15 kg.

 

a. (*) Calculate the process capability ratio, assuming that the lower and upper tolerance limits are 1.1 kg and 1.7 kg respectively. Is Tyler currently able to meet the tolerance limits 99.7% of the time?

 

b. (**) What would the standard deviation have to be for Tyler to achieve Six Sigma quality levels with regard to the weight of the bee packages?

 

c. (**) The average bee weighs 0.1 grams. Use this information to convert the target package weight and tolerance limits into number of bees for Tyler. How might the company use this information to better control the package weights? Should Tyler think about resetting the tolerance limits?

ANSWER:

Process capability ratio Cpk = min ( (USL-)/3, ( - LSL)/3)

Cpk = min ((1.7-1.4)/(3*0.15),(1.4-1.1)/(3*0.15))

Cpk = min (0.667, 0.667)

Cpk = 0.667

 

b. for six sigma the no.of segments between the upper & lower level is 12.

The standard deviation have to be for Tyler to achieve Six Sigma quality levels = (USL-LSL)/12 = (1.7-1.1)/12 = 0.05
