QUESTION:

How has Unilever targeted the black population in Brazil? How does the company’s strategy in Brazil differ from its strategy in other countries? What does your response tell you about Unilever’s overall global marketing strategy?

ANSWER:

The black population in Brazil is targetted by Unilever just the same way it had targetted the Black populations in the USA which was effective previously. However, the marketing strategy backfired because Unilever did not take into account that the blacks in Brazil were socio-culturally different from the Blacks in America. After acknowledging the condition, Unilever then changed its strategy. Later on, the Unilever chalked out a strategy that targetted the black populations in Brazil collectively instead of targetting specific segments of the black population. 

Unilever used an approach of marketing called localized marketing. Unilever's advertisement was entirely a different concept which depicted black skin and white skin as different tones. This shows that Unilever's overall global marketing strategy was based on increased market competition and this has resulted in a globalized marketing strategy.
