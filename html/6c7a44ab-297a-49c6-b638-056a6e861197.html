QUESTION:

Refer to Fig. 4.7 and identify each rock-forming mineral below (Fig. A4.3.1). Write its name below the picture.

ANSWER:

The following are the minerals identified using the characteristic feature(s):

1. Augite - dark green crystals with two sets of cleavage nearly perpendicular to each other 

2. Halite - cubic crystals with three sets of cleavage normal to each other

3. Kaolinite - earth-like clayey texture, powdery and fine-grained mineral

4. Muscovite - colorless micaceous mineral split into thin sheets

5. Chlorite - resembles mica, green colored and split into sheets 

6. Biotite - black mineral with platy, sheet-like habit

7. Gypsum - grayish mineral scratched by a pocket knife

8. Orthoclase - white-brown crystal with two sets of perfect cleavage

9. Dolomite - whitish, curved, crystals with rhombohedral cleavage

10. Calcite - Rhombohedral crystal with three sets of cleavage

11. Hornblende - Dark gray crystals with the characteristic 56 / 124 cleavage angle

12. Olivine - dark green crystals without any cleavage

13. Garnet - reddish brown crystal without cleavages

14. Plagioclase - thin striations on two sets of cleavage

15. Quartz - grayish milky white crystal with glassy texture and curved fractures
