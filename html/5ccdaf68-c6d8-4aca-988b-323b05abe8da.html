QUESTION:

8. Your grandmother has promised to give you $10,000 when you graduate from college. She is expecting you to graduate three years from now. What happens to the present value of this gift if you speed up your graduation by one year and graduate two years from now?

a) remains constant

b) increases

c) decreases

d) becomes negative

e) cannot be determined from the information provided

ANSWER:

a) Present Value concept depends on the concept of time value of money. According to time value of money, money that is available now (immediately) is worth more than the same money that is available after a time. This is because, even if money is received now and kept idle, it will earn interest. Present value is finding the present worth of future moneys received calculated at a specified rate of return. In the given situation, considering a constant rate of return, for example, 4%, the present value of receiving $10,000 after three years would be less than receiving $10,000 after two years. Here too, the same logic applies, the benefit of receiving money sooner. 

In the example taken (4% return), PV of $10,000 at 2 years would be 10,000 x 1/(1.04)^2 = $9,245.56, and 3 years would be 10,000 x 1/(1.04)^3 = $8,889.96.

Therefore, the present value of this gift (b) increases.
