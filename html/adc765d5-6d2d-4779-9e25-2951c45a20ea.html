HCl + H​​​​​​2O​​​​​ H​​​​​​3O+  ​​​​(aq) + Cl- (aq)

100 ml of water = 100 g of water

no of moles of water = 100g/ mol wt

molecular weight of water 18g/mol

so, no of moles of water in 100 ml of water = 100g/18g/mole = 5.5 moles

Since, .2 moles of HCl reacts with only .2 moles of water and forms .2 moles of H3O+  (Hydronium ion). 

100 ml of water contain 0.2 moles of hydronium ion.

1000ml of water contain = 0.2 moles  × 1000/100

= 2 mole/litre

= 2 M

Now, we know concentration of hydronium ion is 2 M 

We also know that 

pH = -log[H+]

so, pH = -log[2]

pH = -0.3

So, pH of the solution is -0.3.
