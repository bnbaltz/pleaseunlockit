Discovery:

Sharing information and evidence in a particular case between the prosecutor and the defense attorney is known as discovery. It enables both the parties of the case not to gain an undue advantage over the other. It upholds the search of truth in the case and does not favor either of the party.

Discovery in civil cases:

Civil cases are mostly related to property crimes and the court has ordered the exchange of information between both the parties. Unlike criminal cases the parties in a civil proceeding can be at an advantageous position with the information available. The aim is to settle the case with full disclosure of facts.

Discovery in criminal cases:

In criminal cases the discovery is less flexible. The scope of discovery for the defense in the information is limited with a view that defendant may try to suppress the evidence. The respective state courts have framed guidelines for type of disclosure of evidences and witnesses.

Reason for the difference in civil and criminal cases with regard to discovery rules:

Criminal cases is given due importance because of its heinous nature. The main aim is to ensure fair trial for the crime committed. If all the information is made available to the defense then there might be an attempt to suppress it. Therefore it is necessary protect certain critical information which otherwise may cause the destruction of strong evidence.
