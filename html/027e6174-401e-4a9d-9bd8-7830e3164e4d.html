If the expected rate of inflation increases, we would expect the required return to increase by a similar amount to derive the constant real rates of return. If the relationship is perfect and investors are accurate, then the difference between the interest rate and the inflation rate would be fairly constant, reflecting the real return on the Treasury bonds. Here the rate of inflation has increased by 3% and the long-term treasury bonds are yielding 7%. If the rate of inflation has increased from 3% to 6%, then the yield on treasury bonds will also increase by 3% which results to 10%.

Similarly the same effect will be there in the coupon bonds. That is the yield of the coupon bonds will also increase by the same amount.

The coupon bonds yield 8% and after the inflation, the bonds will yield (8+3) =11%.
