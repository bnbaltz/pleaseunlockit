ANSWER:

Most bacteria and archaea fall into three shape categories: rod, sphere, and spiral. The rod shaped organisms all arrange themselves singularly, in pairs, or groups with the exception of the spiral shape. Rods form groups as chains. Spheres form groups of four (tetrad), clusters, or chains. A single spiral organism is a vibrio and is shaped like a comma. Spiral pairs and groups appear as a single unit, but gets more tightly and repetitively turned as more organisms join the group as either spirillum or spirochete.
