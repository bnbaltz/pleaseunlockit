hCG refers to the Human Chorionic  Gonadotrophic Hormone. It is a hormone secreted by the cells of the placenta after it forms as a result of fertilization of egg and can be tested to detect pregnancy. Thus it is also called as the pregnancy hormone.

hCG is measured in mIU/ml i.e milli-international units per milliliter.

Levels of hCG can be tested from urine sample or blood sample. Levels of HCG helps to detect the stage of the pregnancy and to determine the development of the foetus.

At initial stages from the last missed periods, i.e after about 2 weeks of conception the HCG hormone levels are detected in blood that gradually increase from 5 mIU/ml to 50 mIU/ml.

As days pass the doubling time of hormone levels become 48 hours to 96 hours.

In the first trimester, hCG levels range from 5 mIU/ml to 253,000 mIU/ml. It is usually high towards the end of the first trimester.

After that it maintains a stable amount or lowers slightly till child birth

In the 2nd trimester HCG levels are from 4,060 to 165,400 mIU/ml. 

In the 3rd trimester,it is about 3,640 117,000 mIU/ml

If hCG levels suddenly lowers to a single digit number, it indicates that a miscarriage can happen.
