ANSWER:

Product costs are direct materials, direct labours and manufacturing overhead.

Whereas period costs mean the other expenses which are not related to manufacturing.

1. Product costs incurred to make 20,000 units.....

   Direct materials                             = 20,000*7    = $ 140,000

   Direct labour                                 = 20,000*4    = $  80,000

   Variable Manufacturing overhead = 20,000*1.5 = $  30,000

   Fixed manufacturing overhead     = 20,000*5    = $ 100,000

   Total product costs                                             = $ 350,000

2. Total amount of period costs incurred to sell 20,000 units...

   Fixed selling Expenses                 = 20,000*3.50 = $ 70,000

   Fixed administrative expenses     = 20,000*2.50 = $ 50,000

   Variable administrative expenses = 20,000*0.50 = $ 10,000

   Sales commission                         = 20,000*1.00 = $ 20,000

   Total period costs                                                 = $ 150,000

3. Total amount of product costs incurred to make 22,000 units....

   Direct materials                            = 22,000*7.00 = $ 154,000

   Direct labour                                 = 22,000*4.00 = $ 88,000

   Variable Manufacturing overhead = 22,000*1.5 = $  33,000

   Fixed manufacturing overhead     = 20,000*5    = $ 100,000 

   Total product costs                                             = $ 375,000

 ( The fixed expense will not change with change of unit of production)

4. Total amount of period costs incurred to sell 18,000 units.....

   Fixed selling Expenses                 = 20,000*3.50 = $ 70,000

   Fixed administrative expenses     = 20,000*2.50 = $ 50,000

   Variable administrative expenses = 18,000*0.50 = $  9,000

   Sales commission                         = 18,000*1.00 = $ 18,000

   Total period costs                                                 = $ 147,000
